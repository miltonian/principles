## Detailed Concerns

**§2-b — Late fee** — "a late fee of $75 applies after the 3rd."
Flat and not prorated: being one day late costs the same as weeks late, and nothing caps how it stacks with other charges. (Lower severity — there is a 3-day grace.)

**§3(a) — Automatic renewal** — "This lease renews automatically for successive twelve-month terms."
Silence or inaction commits you to another full 12-month rent liability. You cannot simply let the lease lapse; you can only exit through the exact steps in §3(b).

**§3(b)-a — Narrow exit window** — "Tenant may decline renewal only by certified written notice delivered no earlier than 120 and no later than 90 days before term end."
Certified notice inside a 30-day window is the "only" way out. Notice a day early, a day late, or by ordinary mail is ineffective. You bear the entire risk of a paperwork misstep.

**§3(b)-b — Void notice → landlord-set rent** — "notice outside this window is void and the lease renews at a rent of Landlord's sole determination."
A single missed deadline binds you for another year AND hands the landlord unilateral pricing power — no cap, no market reference — precisely when you have lost leverage.

**§5 — Full repair/maintenance shift** — "Tenant shall bear the full cost of all repairs and maintenance… (including plumbing, electrical, heating, and appliances) regardless of cause, ordinary wear and tear included, except structural roof failure."
Nearly every repair cost — including major systems and deterioration you didn't cause — falls on you, with no dollar ceiling. Only structural roof failure stays with the landlord.

**§6 — Quiet enjoyment hollowed** — "Tenant is entitled to quiet enjoyment of the premises subject to the terms herein."
"Subject to the terms herein" subordinates your peace and privacy to every other landlord-favorable provision — notably the §7 entry right — draining a seemingly protective covenant of practical force.

**§7 — Unlimited no-notice entry** — "Landlord or its agents may enter the premises at any time without notice for inspection, maintenance, showings, or any business purpose."
No advance warning, no reasonable-hours limit, no right to refuse, and an open-ended catch-all ("any business purpose"). Your privacy at home is effectively unprotected. (Also flagged as an omission: the clause that should set entry procedure supplies none.)

**§8-a — Pet consent at discretion** — "No pets without written consent."
No stated standard for granting consent, so a refusal is neither predictable nor challengeable.

**§8-b — Nonrefundable pet fee** — "approved pets require a $500 nonrefundable fee."
Unrecoverable even if the pet causes no damage, and not credited against the deposit — a pure sunk cost on top of it.

**§9(a) — Deposit forfeiture + damages** — "Deposit of $4,300 is forfeited in full if Tenant vacates before the end of any renewal term for any reason, in addition to any damages owed."
The full deposit (two months' rent) is forfeited for ANY early exit from ANY renewal term, AND you still owe damages on top, with no credit if the unit is re-rented. Combined with §3(a) auto-renewal, each new term re-arms this trigger indefinitely. (Also flagged as an omission: no landlord duty to mitigate / re-rent offset — you can be charged twice for the same period.)

**§9(b) — Slow deposit return** — "Deposit returnable within 60 days of move-out otherwise."
Keeps a large sum out of your hands for two months, just when you need it for a new home. (Also flagged as an omission: no itemized deductions, no accounting, no interest — you cannot verify or contest what is kept.)

**§10 — Mandatory renter's insurance** — "Tenant shall maintain renter's insurance of at least $100,000 liability."
A recurring out-of-pocket cost for the life of the tenancy, at a set minimum, regardless of your own risk assessment.

**§11 — Uncapped mid-term rent increases** — "Landlord may increase rent at any time during the term upon 15 days' notice… at Landlord's sole discretion."
The landlord can raise rent at any time, in any amount, on 15 days' notice — gutting the budgeting certainty a fixed term is meant to provide. (Together with §3(b)-b, flagged as an omission: no rent ceiling exists anywhere, mid-term or at renewal.)

**§12-a — Jury-trial waiver** — "Tenant waives the right to a jury trial."
Channels every dispute to a single decision-maker, removing a protection often relied on against a better-resourced landlord.

**§12-b — Class/collective-action waiver** — "and to participate in any class or collective action against Landlord."
Forces you to pursue even a shared, systemic grievance (e.g., an unlawful fee charged to every tenant) alone — often economically impractical, leaving real harms effectively unremediable.

**§12-c — Landlord-controlled arbitration** — "all disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration before an arbitrator selected and paid for… by Landlord."
Hands your adversary control over the decision-maker, who has a structural financial incentive to favor the party paying them, and removes court access. Caveat: the ellipsis suggests this wording may be truncated in the original — re-verify against the full text.

**§13 — Alterations barred** — "No alterations without written consent."
No stated standard and no exception for minor or reversible changes; you cannot adapt the home even trivially without unreviewable approval.

**§14 — One-way fee shift** — "In any dispute, Tenant shall pay Landlord's attorney's fees and costs regardless of outcome."
You pay the landlord's fees in any dispute even ones you WIN — a result-blind penalty that strongly deters you from asserting or even defending legitimate claims, compounding the §12-c landlord-controlled forum.

**§5/§6 — No habitability guarantee (omission)** — Neither the repairs clause nor the quiet-enjoyment clause affirmatively preserves a guarantee that the home will be livable, and §6 points back to the tenant-adverse body, leaving no stated habitability baseline to invoke if conditions deteriorate.

**§1 — Blank commencement year (omission)** — "Twelve (12) months commencing October 1."
Without the year you cannot compute exact expiry, and therefore cannot reliably calculate the 120–90-day §3(b)-a window — raising your risk of missing that deadline and being locked into an auto-renewed term at landlord-set rent.
