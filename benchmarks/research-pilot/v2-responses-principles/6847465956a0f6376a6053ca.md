## 1. Problem framing & assumptions

This design proceeds under three stated premises, treated as fixed throughout.

- **(a) Input modality — 2D few-channel fluorescence.** Inputs are 2D single- or few-channel frames of shape H×W×C with C small (typically 1–3). Volumetric z-stacks are out of scope unless a user later specifies otherwise. Consequently 2D convolutions suffice, no volumetric processing is warranted, and model capacity concentrates on in-plane context.
- **(b) Supervision — pixel-accurate instance labels.** A pixel-accurate, instance-labeled training set of sufficient size exists or is obtainable. This makes fully supervised learning the operating regime and makes it trainable to regress a learned per-pixel intermediate representation that a downstream step reconstructs into discrete instances.
- **(c) Noise character — Poisson shot plus Gaussian read.** Imaging noise is dominated by signal-dependent Poisson (shot) noise combined with Gaussian read noise. It is heteroscedastic: variance scales with signal intensity, with a persistent read-noise floor even at low signal. It is not additive white/homoscedastic Gaussian noise.

These constraints motivate everything that follows: the model stays lightweight, is framed as a regressor of an intermediate field, and must treat absolute intensity as an unreliable cue.

---

## 2. Intermediate representation & why direct instance-ID output fails

A U-Net cannot emit separated touching instances as a direct dense per-pixel instance-ID map. Instance separation requires two distinct stages: **(a)** an intermediate learned representation the network *regresses*, and **(b)** a separate decoding step that converts that representation into discrete instance labels. A direct dense instance-ID output is ill-posed for four reasons:

1. **Permutation-arbitrariness.** Instance IDs are labels, not measurements — relabeling "cell 3" as "cell 7" leaves the image unchanged. The target is invariant to label ordering, so there is no consistent pixel→ID function to regress; the loss would penalize correct segmentations that merely permute IDs.
2. **Variable, unbounded count.** The object count differs per image with no fixed upper bound, so no fixed-width output can enumerate it.
3. **Fixed-channel softmax cannot scale.** A K-channel classification head caps the representable object count at K and still inherits the permutation problem.
4. **Semantic segmentation merges neighbors.** A foreground/background head labels touching cells as one connected blob; at shared seams the objects are indistinguishable — exactly the case we must resolve. Under premise (c)'s heteroscedastic noise, intensity at those seams is unreliable, so thresholding cannot re-split them.

The network must therefore predict a *count-agnostic, geometry-encoding field*, and instances must emerge only at decode time.

**Candidate representations.**

| Representation (U-Net regresses) | Decode step | Strength | Failure mode under (a)–(c) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Semantic mask + **boundary/contour** channel | Subtract boundary, connected-components / watershed | Sharp separation when boundary visible | ~1-px boundary fragile under Poisson+read noise; dim edges vanish; cannot split objects with no visible seam |
| **Distance transform** (normalized EDT) | Distance maxima as seeds → marker-controlled watershed | Smooth, noise-averaged, count-agnostic | Merged maxima on branched organelles; star-convex variants break on non-convex shapes |
| **Per-pixel flow / vector field** | Follow flow to fixed-point sinks; cluster convergent pixels | Dense, redundant, noise-averaged; handles branched shapes; splits touching objects with no visible seam | Center-finding sensitive to field noise; needs a foreground mask |

**Primary choice: per-pixel flow field + foreground channel.** Every interior pixel points toward its own object's center, so two adjacent objects have *divergent* flow at the seam even when no boundary is visible — separation is defined by geometry, not by a detectable edge. The flow is a smooth, spatially-supported field whose supervision is dense and redundant, averaging over heteroscedastic noise rather than depending on a single dim pixel. The field is count-agnostic; instances appear only when the decoder clusters convergent pixels. And because the field routes pixels along medial geometry, it accommodates convex cells and irregular/branched organelles far better than convex-assuming radial-distance schemes.

**Handoff contract.** The regression head emits horizontal flow `f_x`, vertical flow `f_y`, and foreground probability `p_fg` (predicted as a logit). Targets are derived deterministically from the pixel-accurate masks: for each instance, take the normalized gradient of an intra-object diffusion or distance-transform field; `p_fg` is the union of all masks. Flow is defined only inside foreground. This exact representation is what the heads emit, what the loss supervises, and what the decoder consumes.

## 3. Architecture variant & mechanism justification

**Variant declaration.** We adopt an **attention-gated residual U-Net**: residual encoder/decoder blocks, additive attention gates on every skip connection, and a single global self-attention layer at the lowest-resolution bottleneck. Every element is justified *only* by a stated property of this problem — the signal-dependent Poisson+read noise of premise (c) and the presence of touching/overlapping instances. No component is claimed to be better in the abstract.

**1. Residual encoder/decoder blocks — variance-reducing depth without edge erasure.** The noise is Poisson shot noise (variance grows with signal) on a Gaussian read-noise floor, and is approximately independent across pixels. Local averaging is therefore the correct estimator to lower per-pixel variance — convolutional stacks exploit this pixel-independence to trade spatial support for noise reduction. But averaging deep enough to suppress heteroscedastic variance also blurs the object boundaries downstream separation depends on. The residual identity path resolves this tension: the skip-through preserves high-frequency boundary signal while the convolutional branch does the variance-reducing averaging. Depth buys denoising; the identity path buys boundary preservation.

**2. Attention gates on skip connections — intensity/context-adaptive suppression.** Because noise variance scales with intensity above a read floor, the worst SNR sits in the *dim inter-cell gaps* — precisely where instances must be separated. A raw skip forwards encoder activations verbatim, including spurious noise-fired activations the decoder can mistake for structure. Additive attention gates compute a context-conditioned multiplicative gate (informed by the coarser decoder signal, already denoised through more averaging) that down-weights low-confidence low-SNR skip activations while passing sharp boundaries at full strength. The gate is *adaptive*, not a fixed threshold — appropriate because under signal-dependent noise no single intensity cutoff is valid across the dynamic range.

**3. Bottleneck global self-attention — long-range instance-ownership reasoning.** Overlapping cells create locally ambiguous pixels: a small window around an overlap cannot tell which instance a pixel belongs to. Resolving ownership needs long-range reasoning tying an ambiguous pixel to the distant, unambiguous body of its parent instance. A single global self-attention layer supplies this all-pairs interaction. Its placement is deliberate: self-attention carries no local-averaging inductive bias and hence no denoising prior, so applying it to noisy high-resolution features would let Poisson/read noise leak into the routing. We place it at the bottleneck so it operates *downstream* of the convolutional denoising path — on already variance-stabilized features — and at the lowest resolution, where all-pairs attention is affordable.

**Rejection of a pure-transformer encoder.** A fully transformer-based encoder is rejected *for this problem specifically*: self-attention lacks the local-averaging inductive bias and cannot cheaply reduce the per-pixel Poisson variance that dominates the input; it would have to learn denoising from data without the structural prior convolution provides for free. We confine self-attention to the single bottleneck layer where its long-range strength is needed and its lack of a denoising prior is harmless.

| Component | Mechanism | Problem property it answers |
|---|---|---|
| Residual blocks | Variance-reducing local averaging; identity path preserves boundaries | Heteroscedastic shot+read noise; depth without edge erasure |
| Attention gates on skips | Context-adaptive multiplicative gating of low-SNR skips | Worst SNR in dim inter-cell gaps; no valid fixed threshold |
| Bottleneck self-attention | Long-range ownership reasoning on denoised features | Locally ambiguous overlap pixels |
| (Rejected) pure-transformer encoder | Lacks local-averaging bias to cut Poisson variance | Poisson+read noise demands a convolutional denoising prior first |

The backbone's job is to deliver a boundary-sharp, noise-suppressed flow field; that sharpness is the deliberate handoff on which the decoder relies.

## 4. Model code

The model returns a dict `{"flow": (B,2,H,W), "foreground_logits": (B,1,H,W)}`. The head names map one-to-one to the loss inputs (`pred_flow ← "flow"`, `pred_fg_logits ← "foreground_logits"`) and to the decoder input, which consumes a stacked array `[f_x, f_y, p_fg]` where `p_fg = sigmoid(foreground_logits)`. The same three tensors flow unchanged through model → loss → decoder. The flow head is regression with no output activation (`f_x, f_y` are signed, unbounded direction components); the foreground head emits logits, so the loss treats them with different objectives.

```python
"""
Attention-gated residual U-Net that regresses a per-pixel flow field
(f_x, f_y) + foreground logits. Run `python model.py` for a shape check.
"""
import torch
import torch.nn as nn
import torch.nn.functional as F


def gn(num_channels, max_groups=8):
    """GroupNorm (not BatchNorm) -- microscopy batches are small, so per-sample
    normalization is more stable than batch statistics."""
    groups = min(max_groups, num_channels)
    while num_channels % groups != 0:
        groups -= 1
    return nn.GroupNorm(groups, num_channels)


class ResidualConvBlock(nn.Module):
    """Two 3x3 convs (GroupNorm + GELU) with an identity/1x1 residual path.
    The conv branch performs variance-reducing local averaging that exploits
    pixel-independence of Poisson+read noise, while the identity path preserves
    boundary signal so depth denoises without eroding edges."""
    def __init__(self, in_ch, out_ch):
        super().__init__()
        self.conv1 = nn.Conv2d(in_ch, out_ch, 3, padding=1)
        self.norm1 = gn(out_ch)
        self.conv2 = nn.Conv2d(out_ch, out_ch, 3, padding=1)
        self.norm2 = gn(out_ch)
        self.act = nn.GELU()
        self.proj = nn.Conv2d(in_ch, out_ch, 1) if in_ch != out_ch else nn.Identity()

    def forward(self, x):
        idn = self.proj(x)
        h = self.act(self.norm1(self.conv1(x)))
        h = self.norm2(self.conv2(h))
        return self.act(h + idn)          # identity path preserves boundaries


class AttentionGate(nn.Module):
    """Additive attention gate on a skip connection. The coarser, more-denoised
    decoder signal `gate` computes a context-conditioned coefficient that
    down-weights low-SNR spurious skip activations (worst in dim inter-cell
    gaps) while passing sharp boundaries. It MULTIPLIES rather than concatenates,
    so a noise-fired activation can be attenuated toward zero."""
    def __init__(self, skip_ch, gate_ch, inter_ch=None):
        super().__init__()
        inter_ch = inter_ch or max(skip_ch // 2, 1)
        self.theta = nn.Conv2d(skip_ch, inter_ch, 1)
        self.phi = nn.Conv2d(gate_ch, inter_ch, 1)
        self.psi = nn.Conv2d(inter_ch, 1, 1)
        self.act = nn.GELU()

    def forward(self, skip, gate):
        g = F.interpolate(gate, size=skip.shape[-2:], mode="bilinear",
                          align_corners=False)                 # align to skip
        a = self.act(self.theta(skip) + self.phi(g))
        alpha = torch.sigmoid(self.psi(a))                     # (B,1,H,W) coeffs
        return skip * alpha                                    # multiplicative


class SelfAttention2d(nn.Module):
    """Single global multi-head self-attention over the bottleneck feature map.
    All-pairs interaction gives long-range instance-ownership reasoning to
    disambiguate overlap pixels; placed at the bottleneck so it runs on
    already-denoised features (it has no local-averaging prior)."""
    def __init__(self, channels, num_heads=4):
        super().__init__()
        assert channels % num_heads == 0
        self.norm = gn(channels)
        self.qkv = nn.Conv2d(channels, channels * 3, 1)
        self.proj = nn.Conv2d(channels, channels, 1)
        self.num_heads = num_heads

    def forward(self, x):
        B, C, H, W = x.shape
        h = self.norm(x)
        qkv = self.qkv(h).reshape(B, 3, self.num_heads, C // self.num_heads, H * W)
        q, k, v = qkv[:, 0], qkv[:, 1], qkv[:, 2]              # (B,heads,dh,N)
        q = q.transpose(-2, -1)                                # (B,heads,N,dh)
        attn = torch.softmax((q @ k) / (k.shape[-2] ** 0.5), dim=-1)
        out = attn @ v.transpose(-2, -1)                       # (B,heads,N,dh)
        out = out.transpose(-2, -1).reshape(B, C, H, W)
        return x + self.proj(out)                              # residual


class AttnResUNetFlow(nn.Module):
    """Attention-gated residual U-Net with a bottleneck self-attention layer.
      - flow_head:  2ch regression, no activation  -> (f_x, f_y)
      - fg_head:    1ch logits (sigmoid applied in the loss) -> p_fg
    A separate decoding step turns these into instance IDs."""
    def __init__(self, in_channels=1, base_width=32, depth=4, attn_heads=4):
        super().__init__()
        self.depth = depth
        widths = [base_width * (2 ** i) for i in range(depth + 1)]

        self.enc_blocks = nn.ModuleList()
        self.downs = nn.ModuleList()
        prev = in_channels
        for i in range(depth):
            self.enc_blocks.append(ResidualConvBlock(prev, widths[i]))
            self.downs.append(nn.Conv2d(widths[i], widths[i], 2, stride=2))
            prev = widths[i]

        self.bottleneck = ResidualConvBlock(widths[depth - 1], widths[depth])
        self.attn = SelfAttention2d(widths[depth], num_heads=attn_heads)

        self.ups = nn.ModuleList()
        self.gates = nn.ModuleList()
        self.dec_blocks = nn.ModuleList()
        for i in reversed(range(depth)):
            self.ups.append(nn.ConvTranspose2d(widths[i + 1], widths[i], 2, stride=2))
            self.gates.append(AttentionGate(skip_ch=widths[i], gate_ch=widths[i + 1]))
            self.dec_blocks.append(ResidualConvBlock(widths[i] * 2, widths[i]))

        self.flow_head = nn.Conv2d(base_width, 2, 1)   # (f_x, f_y) regression
        self.fg_head = nn.Conv2d(base_width, 1, 1)     # foreground logits

    def forward(self, x):
        skips = []
        h = x
        for i in range(self.depth):
            h = self.enc_blocks[i](h)
            skips.append(h)
            h = self.downs[i](h)
        h = self.bottleneck(h)
        h = self.attn(h)                               # long-range ownership
        for j, i in enumerate(reversed(range(self.depth))):
            gate = h                                   # coarser, more-denoised
            gated = self.gates[j](skips[i], gate)      # attenuate noisy skip
            up = self.ups[j](h)
            up = F.interpolate(up, size=gated.shape[-2:], mode="bilinear",
                               align_corners=False)    # arbitrary even sizes
            h = self.dec_blocks[j](torch.cat([up, gated], dim=1))
        return {"flow": self.flow_head(h),
                "foreground_logits": self.fg_head(h)}


if __name__ == "__main__":
    torch.manual_seed(0)
    model = AttnResUNetFlow(in_channels=1, base_width=32, depth=4, attn_heads=4)
    n = sum(p.numel() for p in model.parameters())
    print(f"parameters: {n/1e6:.2f}M")
    for shape in [(2, 1, 256, 256), (1, 1, 192, 320)]:
        out = model(torch.randn(*shape))
        print(shape, "->", {k: tuple(v.shape) for k, v in out.items()})
        assert out["flow"].shape[1] == 2
        assert out["foreground_logits"].shape[1] == 1
        assert out["flow"].shape[-2:] == shape[-2:]
    print("shape-check OK")
```

`F.interpolate` on every upsampled feature makes the model robust to arbitrary even input sizes (e.g. 192×320), a practical necessity for tiled microscopy fields.

## 5. Loss design & code

The loss supervises **only** the intermediate representation — flow `(f_x, f_y)` + foreground `p_fg`. It never predicts instance IDs; splitting touching objects happens in the separate decode step. The objective's job is to make the flow *divergent at seams* and the foreground *complete under noise*, so downstream decoding has a clean field to invert.

**Term 1 — Noise-aware flow regression.** We regress `(f_x, f_y)` with a cosine *direction* term (direction is what the decoder's dynamics follow) plus a Smooth-L1 *magnitude* term, **masked to ground-truth foreground** (flow is undefined on background), and precision-weighted for the true noise model. The noise lives on the *input* image `s(x)`, where a scientific-camera pixel has variance `Var[I] ≈ a·s(x) + b`: the `a·s(x)` term is signal-dependent Poisson noise scaled by gain `a`, and `b` is the Gaussian read-noise-variance floor. This is not homoscedastic Gaussian, so a plain L2/MSE — which implicitly assumes uniform per-pixel variance — is *mismatched*: it over-weights bright, high-variance pixels whose targets are least trustworthy and under-weights dim inter-object regions that matter most for separation. We correct this by weighting each pixel by its inverse variance `1/(a·s(x)+b)`, the maximum-likelihood weighting under a Poisson+Gaussian model. The parameters `a` (gain) and `b` (read-noise variance) are camera constants recoverable from a photon-transfer calibration; defaults `a=1.0, b=25.0` are exposed as constructor args. An equivalent alternative (stated, not double-applied) is to apply a generalized Anscombe transform to the input upstream, after which residual noise is ≈ unit-variance Gaussian and unweighted L2 becomes approximately correct; we choose loss-side precision weighting to keep raw intensities available to the encoder.

**Term 2 — Separation term.** The representation has no boundary channel, so seams are sharpened two ways. **(a)** A U-Net inter-object weight map `w_border(x) = wc + w0·exp(−(d1+d2)²/2σ²)` (with `d1, d2` the distances to the nearest and second-nearest instance) up-weights the thin gap between touching cells, forcing capacity onto the pixels that decide the split. **(b)** A flow spatial-gradient consistency term penalizes mismatch of the finite-difference gradients of the flow, so the network cannot minimize loss with a soft, blurred sign-transition across the seam — the sharp divergence the decoder keys on becomes an explicit target. Under Poisson+Gaussian noise a smooth flow is the "easy" degenerate solution; the gradient term makes the sharp divergence an explicit optimization target.

**Term 3 — Foreground term.** Foreground is sparse, so we combine Dice (imbalance-robust overlap) with a focal term (down-weights easy background, focuses on dim hard edges). `p_fg` bounds object extent for the decoder; Dice+focal keeps overwhelming background from washing out the gradient on dim low-SNR edges. (Tversky is a drop-in if false negatives on dim structure must be penalized harder — set `α<0.5`.)

**Composite.** `L = λ_flow·L_flow + λ_grad·L_grad + λ_fg·L_fg`, defaults `λ_flow=1.0, λ_grad=0.5, λ_fg=1.0`. Flow regression and foreground are the two primary decoder inputs (equal footing); the gradient term is a *regularizer* on the flow at half weight — enough to sharpen seams, not so strong it fights the direction term or amplifies noise into the gradient estimate. All weights are constructor args so they can be retuned to the cell-vs-organelle morphology mix.

```python
import torch
import torch.nn as nn
import torch.nn.functional as F

# ============================================================================
# TARGET-SIDE PREPROCESSING (numpy / scipy). Runs ONCE per training sample on
# CPU to build the U-Net inter-object border weight map from the instance mask.
# ============================================================================
import numpy as np
from scipy.ndimage import distance_transform_edt


def unet_border_weight_map(instance_mask: np.ndarray,
                           w0: float = 10.0, sigma: float = 5.0,
                           wc: float = 1.0) -> np.ndarray:
    """w(x) = wc + w0 * exp( -(d1(x) + d2(x))^2 / (2 * sigma^2) )
    d1, d2 = distances to the nearest / second-nearest instance. Large in the
    narrow background gap between two touching cells -> Term 2(a)."""
    instance_mask = np.asarray(instance_mask)
    H, W = instance_mask.shape
    ids = [i for i in np.unique(instance_mask) if i != 0]
    if len(ids) == 0:
        return np.full((H, W), wc, dtype=np.float32)

    dts = np.zeros((len(ids), H, W), dtype=np.float32)
    for k, i in enumerate(ids):
        dts[k] = distance_transform_edt(instance_mask != i)

    dts.sort(axis=0)
    d1 = dts[0]
    d2 = dts[1] if len(ids) > 1 else np.full((H, W), 1e6, dtype=np.float32)

    w = wc + w0 * np.exp(-((d1 + d2) ** 2) / (2.0 * sigma ** 2))
    w[instance_mask > 0] = wc             # emphasis is on inter-cell gaps
    return w.astype(np.float32)


# ============================================================================
# LOSS (torch only). Supervises the representation: flow (f_x, f_y) + p_fg.
# ============================================================================
def _spatial_grads(t: torch.Tensor):
    """Finite-difference spatial gradients along x and y (edge-padded)."""
    dx = F.pad(t[:, :, :, 1:] - t[:, :, :, :-1], (0, 1, 0, 0))
    dy = F.pad(t[:, :, 1:, :] - t[:, :, :-1, :], (0, 0, 0, 1))
    return dx, dy


class TouchingInstanceFlowLoss(nn.Module):
    """Composite loss for flow + foreground under Poisson+Gaussian noise.
    Camera noise variance model:  Var[I] ~= a * signal + b
        a = gain (electrons per ADU), b = read-noise variance (ADU^2)."""

    def __init__(self,
                 a: float = 1.0, b: float = 25.0,
                 lambda_flow: float = 1.0,
                 lambda_grad: float = 0.5,
                 lambda_fg: float = 1.0,
                 focal_gamma: float = 2.0, focal_alpha: float = 0.5,
                 focal_weight: float = 1.0,
                 dice_smooth: float = 1.0, eps: float = 1e-8):
        super().__init__()
        self.a, self.b = a, b
        self.lambda_flow, self.lambda_grad, self.lambda_fg = (
            lambda_flow, lambda_grad, lambda_fg)
        self.focal_gamma, self.focal_alpha, self.focal_weight = (
            focal_gamma, focal_alpha, focal_weight)
        self.dice_smooth, self.eps = dice_smooth, eps

    def flow_loss(self, pred_flow, tgt_flow, w):
        dot = (pred_flow * tgt_flow).sum(dim=1, keepdim=True)
        pn = pred_flow.norm(dim=1, keepdim=True)
        tn = tgt_flow.norm(dim=1, keepdim=True)
        cos = dot / (pn * tn + self.eps)
        cos_term = 1.0 - cos
        mag_term = F.smooth_l1_loss(
            pred_flow, tgt_flow, reduction='none').mean(dim=1, keepdim=True)
        per_pixel = cos_term + mag_term
        return (w * per_pixel).sum() / (w.sum() + self.eps)

    def grad_loss(self, pred_flow, tgt_flow, border_w):
        pdx, pdy = _spatial_grads(pred_flow)
        tdx, tdy = _spatial_grads(tgt_flow)
        diff = (pdx - tdx).abs().mean(dim=1, keepdim=True) + \
               (pdy - tdy).abs().mean(dim=1, keepdim=True)
        return (border_w * diff).sum() / (border_w.sum() + self.eps)

    def fg_loss(self, pred_fg_logits, tgt_fg):
        p = torch.sigmoid(pred_fg_logits)
        g = tgt_fg
        inter = (p * g).sum()
        dice = 1.0 - (2.0 * inter + self.dice_smooth) / (
            p.sum() + g.sum() + self.dice_smooth)
        logp = F.logsigmoid(pred_fg_logits)
        log1mp = F.logsigmoid(-pred_fg_logits)
        focal = -(self.focal_alpha * (1 - p) ** self.focal_gamma * g * logp
                  + (1 - self.focal_alpha) * p ** self.focal_gamma
                  * (1 - g) * log1mp)
        return dice + self.focal_weight * focal.mean()

    def forward(self, pred_flow, pred_fg_logits,
                tgt_flow, tgt_fg, signal, border_w):
        fg_mask = (tgt_fg > 0.5).float()                    # flow only on fg
        noise_prec = 1.0 / (self.a * signal + self.b)       # inverse variance
        w = fg_mask * noise_prec * border_w                 # combined weight

        l_flow = self.flow_loss(pred_flow, tgt_flow, w)
        l_grad = self.grad_loss(pred_flow, tgt_flow, border_w * fg_mask)
        l_fg = self.fg_loss(pred_fg_logits, tgt_fg)

        total = (self.lambda_flow * l_flow
                 + self.lambda_grad * l_grad
                 + self.lambda_fg * l_fg)
        return total, {"flow": l_flow.item(), "grad": l_grad.item(),
                       "fg": l_fg.item(), "total": total.item()}


if __name__ == "__main__":
    torch.manual_seed(0)
    np.random.seed(0)
    B, H, W = 2, 64, 64

    border_np = np.zeros((B, 1, H, W), dtype=np.float32)
    fg_np = np.zeros((B, 1, H, W), dtype=np.float32)
    for bi in range(B):
        mask = np.zeros((H, W), dtype=np.int32)
        mask[10:30, 10:30] = 1          # two touching square "cells"
        mask[10:30, 30:50] = 2
        border_np[bi, 0] = unet_border_weight_map(mask)
        fg_np[bi, 0] = (mask > 0).astype(np.float32)

    border_w = torch.from_numpy(border_np)
    tgt_fg = torch.from_numpy(fg_np)

    pred_flow = torch.randn(B, 2, H, W, requires_grad=True)
    pred_fg_logits = torch.randn(B, 1, H, W, requires_grad=True)
    tgt_flow = F.normalize(torch.randn(B, 2, H, W), dim=1) * tgt_fg
    signal = torch.rand(B, 1, H, W) * 1000.0        # intensities in ADU

    loss_fn = TouchingInstanceFlowLoss(a=1.0, b=25.0)
    total, parts = loss_fn(pred_flow, pred_fg_logits,
                           tgt_flow, tgt_fg, signal, border_w)
    total.backward()
    print("Loss terms:", parts)
```

From each pixel-accurate instance mask, the flow targets `(f_x, f_y)` are the normalized gradient of an intra-object diffusion (or distance-transform) field, and `p_fg` is the union of masks; `signal` is the raw frame and `border_w` is precomputed by `unet_border_weight_map`. Because the loss trains the field to be foreground-complete and sharply divergent across seams, the decoder can route touching pixels to distinct attractors and split objects that share no visible boundary.

## 6. Postprocessing / decoding pipeline

**Why decoding, not the network, produces instance IDs.** The backbone regresses the flow field `(f_x, f_y)` plus foreground `p_fg` and emits no instance-ID map — it cannot, for the four reasons in §2. **The separation of touching objects therefore happens here.** This stage inverts the flow field by routing each foreground pixel to the fixed-point attractor (sink) of its parent object, so objects that touch with no visible intensity edge are still split because their interiors flow *diverge* at the seam.

**Step-by-step decode (flow-following).**

1. **Pre-smooth the predicted maps.** A small Gaussian/median filter on `f_x, f_y, p_fg` stabilizes integration trajectories so residual noise does not scatter pixels into spurious sinks.
2. **Foreground gating.** Threshold `p_fg > τ_fg`; integrate flow only inside the mask, confining dynamics to real objects and excluding background noise.
3. **Integrate the flow (Euler dynamics).** For each foreground pixel take `n_iter` fixed steps `p ← p + h·flow(p)` (bilinear-sampled). Interior pixels of one object converge to a common sink; an adjacent object's pixels converge to a different sink — this is the actual split.
4. **Cluster by convergence point.** Histogram final coordinates; smoothed local-density maxima are the attractors; pixels sharing a basin share an instance ID. One sink = one instance; count emerges at decode time.
5. **Label, mask, clean.** Assign IDs, re-mask by foreground, fill holes, remove sub-threshold objects.

**Tunable hyperparameters (defaults for low-SNR data).**

| Parameter | Effect | Default (noisy) |
|---|---|---|
| `τ_fg` foreground threshold | Higher → fewer/smaller objects | **0.5** (conservative) |
| Pre-smoothing σ | Higher → fewer spurious sinks, risk merging | **σ≈1–2 px** |
| `n_iter` integration steps | Too few → incomplete convergence (over-seg) | **≥200** |
| Step size `h` | Larger → faster but overshoot | **1.0 px** |
| Attractor min-distance / bin size | Larger → merges nearby sinks (under-seg) | **≈ min cell radius** |
| Min object area | Higher → removes speckle (under-seg risk) | **15–50 px²** |
| Hole-fill / boundary-erosion | Cleans mask topology | fill on; erosion 0–1 px |

Defaults lean conservative because heteroscedastic noise makes intensity unreliable and speckle readily spawns false instances.

**Handling overlap and noise.** Genuine overlap is handled by the flow's geometry: even where two organelles' signals superimpose, the field assigns each pixel to the nearer center and sink-clustering resolves ownership (ambiguous pixels take the majority-vote basin of their neighborhood). Noise is suppressed at three points — map pre-smoothing averages per-pixel jitter, foreground gating excludes background shot noise, and the minimum-area filter deletes micro-clusters a spike might seed. None relies on a global intensity threshold, honoring premise (c). **Convention alert:** in the code below `fy` drives rows and `fx` drives columns; the loss must derive targets with the same axis convention or the decoder routes pixels wrong.

```python
import numpy as np
from scipy.ndimage import gaussian_filter, binary_fill_holes, map_coordinates
from skimage.measure import label as cc_label
from skimage.morphology import remove_small_objects

def _sample_flow(fx, fy, ys, xs):
    coords = np.vstack([ys, xs])
    return (map_coordinates(fx, coords, order=1, mode="nearest"),
            map_coordinates(fy, coords, order=1, mode="nearest"))

def follow_flow(fx, fy, mask, n_iter=200, h=1.0):
    ys, xs = np.nonzero(mask)
    ys = ys.astype(np.float64); xs = xs.astype(np.float64)
    H, W = mask.shape
    for _ in range(n_iter):
        dy, dx = _sample_flow(fx, fy, ys, xs)     # fy drives rows, fx cols
        ys = np.clip(ys + h * dy, 0, H - 1)
        xs = np.clip(xs + h * dx, 0, W - 1)
    return ys, xs

def cluster_sinks(ys, xs, shape, bin_sigma=1.0):
    H, W = shape
    hist = np.zeros(shape, dtype=np.float32)
    np.add.at(hist, (np.round(ys).astype(int), np.round(xs).astype(int)), 1.0)
    hist = gaussian_filter(hist, bin_sigma)
    seeds = cc_label(hist > (0.5 * hist.max()))    # connected basins = instances
    return seeds[np.round(ys).astype(int), np.round(xs).astype(int)]

def decode_instances(pred, tau_fg=0.5, smooth_sigma=1.0,
                     n_iter=200, h=1.0, min_area=20):
    """pred : array (3, H, W) = [f_x, f_y, p_fg] (p_fg = sigmoid of logits)."""
    fx, fy, pfg = pred
    fx = gaussian_filter(fx, smooth_sigma)
    fy = gaussian_filter(fy, smooth_sigma)
    pfg = gaussian_filter(pfg, smooth_sigma)
    mask = binary_fill_holes(pfg > tau_fg)
    labels = np.zeros(mask.shape, dtype=np.int32)
    if not mask.any():
        return labels
    ys, xs = follow_flow(fx, fy, mask, n_iter=n_iter, h=h)
    pix_ids = cluster_sinks(ys, xs, mask.shape)
    yy, xx = np.nonzero(mask)
    labels[yy, xx] = pix_ids
    labels = remove_small_objects(labels, min_size=min_area)
    return labels
```

**Failure modes & validation.** Over-segmentation (too many sinks): raise `smooth_sigma`/attractor min-distance, increase `n_iter`. Under-segmentation (merged instances): lower attractor bin/min-distance, reduce smoothing. Noise instances: raise `min_area` and `τ_fg`. Validate with instance-aware metrics — **Aggregated Jaccard Index (AJI)** or **Panoptic Quality** for split/merge behavior, and **AP at multiple IoU thresholds** (0.5–0.9) for detection quality — tuning each hyperparameter against the metric it moves. The decoder consumes exactly the network heads — flow `f_x, f_y` (the model's `"flow"` tensor) and `p_fg` (`sigmoid` of `"foreground_logits"`) — and emits a 2D integer instance-label map with background = 0 and one unique ID per object.

## 7. Comparison to prior models

We compare the proposed design — an attention-gated residual U-Net that regresses a flow field `(f_x, f_y)` + foreground `p_fg` and decodes by flow-following to sinks — against **Cellpose**, **StarDist**, and **Mask R-CNN**. Because our design is flow-based, the Cellpose contrast is central; StarDist and Mask R-CNN bracket the polygon-regression and detect-then-segment paradigms.

| Model | (a) Separation mechanism | (b) Shape assumptions | (c) Noise robustness | (d) Supervision needs |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Proposed** | Regress dense flow → integrate to **sinks** → cluster | Shape-agnostic; routes along medial geometry | Specialized for Poisson+read: residual averaging + attention-gated skips + precision-weighted loss | Instance masks; flow+fg auto-derived |
| **Cellpose** | Predict flow → follow to **sinks**, group by sink | Largely shape-agnostic | Smooth flow noise-averaged; generalist backbone not tuned for extreme low-SNR | Instance masks; flows auto-derived; ships pretrained models |
| **StarDist** | **Star-convex** radial distances + probability → **NMS** | Assumes star-convex; poor on branched organelles | Radial distances degrade on noisy dim edges | Instance masks; star-distances auto-derived |
| **Mask R-CNN** | Detect boxes (**region proposals**) → mask **per proposal** | Flexible within box; struggles when boxes crowd | Detector can miss dim objects; mask inherits proposal errors | Instance masks **plus boxes** |

*Terminology:* a **sink** is a fixed-point attractor where flow converges; **star-convex** means every boundary point is reachable from the center by an unobstructed straight ray; a **region proposal** is a candidate bounding box; **NMS** (non-maximum suppression) greedily discards overlapping lower-confidence candidates.

**(a) Mechanism.** Our design sits in the *same flow-to-sinks family as Cellpose*: it integrates `(f_x, f_y)` inside `p_fg` and clusters convergent pixels, splitting touching objects because interior flow diverges at the shared seam even without an intensity edge. StarDist prunes overlapping star-convex candidate polygons by NMS; Mask R-CNN delegates separation to box detection. We claim no mechanistic advantage over Cellpose here.

**(b) Shape.** StarDist pays a structural price: its star-convex parameterization cannot represent concave, elongated, or branched organelles occluded from any single center. The proposed design inherits Cellpose's shape-agnosticism because flow routes along medial geometry. Its honest residual limitation is shared with Cellpose — extremely elongated shapes can produce unstable/multiple sinks, requiring the §6 tuning.

**(c) Noise.** Under Poisson+read noise, StarDist's crisp radial distances weaken where dim edges are buried in shot noise, and Mask R-CNN's detector can miss dim objects (no box → no mask). Cellpose's dense flow is more noise-tolerant than crisp boundary classification because the smooth target averages over per-pixel noise — but its generalist backbone is not specialized for extreme low-SNR fluorescence. This is where the proposed design earns its **conditional** advantage, only through cited mechanism: residual variance-reducing averaging with a boundary-preserving identity path, attention gates suppressing low-SNR skips in dim inter-cell gaps, bottleneck self-attention reasoning on denoised features, and inverse-variance `1/(a·s+b)` flow weighting rather than plain MSE. These are noise-regime specializations of Cellpose's paradigm, not universal superiority.

**(d) Supervision.** All four require pixel-accurate masks. Cellpose and StarDist derive targets automatically; Mask R-CNN additionally needs boxes (trivially derivable). One practical asymmetry favors the baselines: Cellpose ships generalist pretrained models, whereas a fresh flow-based design must be trained on labeled data (or warm-started from a Cellpose-style prior) before its noise-robustness benefits materialize — a genuine deployment cost, not a modeling advantage.

## 8. Synthesis: how the pipeline holds together end-to-end

The value of this design is not any single block but the *contract* that runs unbroken from raw frame to labeled instances. This section traces that contract explicitly and explains why each seam is placed where it is.

**The single load-bearing representation.** Everything downstream of the encoder is organized around three tensors: `f_x`, `f_y`, and `p_fg`. The model's `flow_head` emits `(f_x, f_y)` as raw regression; its `fg_head` emits `p_fg` as a logit. The loss consumes `pred_flow ← "flow"` and `pred_fg_logits ← "foreground_logits"`, applying `sigmoid` internally only for the Dice/focal foreground term while keeping the flow un-activated. The decoder consumes the stacked array `[f_x, f_y, p_fg]`, where `p_fg = sigmoid(foreground_logits)`. Because all three stages name the *same* three quantities, an integration bug can only enter through two well-marked seams: the sign/axis convention (`fy` drives rows, `fx` drives columns — the loss must derive targets identically) and the sigmoid boundary (the model emits logits; only the loss and the decoder-input packaging apply the sigmoid). Both are called out at their point of use.

**Why the noise model threads through three stages, not one.** Premise (c)'s heteroscedastic Poisson+read noise is not "handled" in a single place; it is answered redundantly at every stage where it can do damage. In the *architecture*, residual blocks apply variance-reducing local averaging while the identity path spares boundaries, and attention gates suppress noise-fired skip activations in exactly the dim inter-cell gaps where SNR is worst. In the *loss*, inverse-variance weighting `1/(a·s+b)` down-weights bright high-variance pixels that a plain MSE would over-trust, and the border weight map plus gradient-consistency term force the network to spend capacity precisely on the seams that decide a split. In *postprocessing*, pre-smoothing, foreground gating, and area filtering suppress residual field jitter so a single noise spike cannot spawn a false instance. No stage relies on a global intensity threshold, because under signal-dependent noise no single intensity cutoff is valid across the dynamic range. This redundancy is deliberate: because absolute intensity is untrustworthy, the design leans on *geometry* (divergent flow at seams) and *statistics* (dense, redundant, noise-averaged supervision) instead.

**Why the two-stage regress-then-decode split is non-negotiable.** The four impossibility arguments in §2 — permutation-arbitrariness, unbounded count, fixed-channel caps, and semantic-blob merging — are not stylistic preferences; they are the reason the network *must* stay count-agnostic and the reason instances *must* emerge only at decode time. The flow field is the minimal representation that satisfies all four: it is permutation-free (there is no ID to permute), count-free (a field has no cardinality), unbounded (sinks are discovered, not enumerated), and seam-splitting (divergence, not edge detection, separates touching objects). The decoder's flow-following is the inverse operation that turns that geometry back into a bounded, integer-labeled map. Splitting the problem this way is what lets the same architecture handle two round touching cells and a branched, non-convex organelle without changing a single output channel.

**How the pieces earn their keep against the baselines.** The comparison in §7 is deliberately narrow about where the design wins. It shares Cellpose's separation mechanism outright and claims nothing there. It beats StarDist only on non-convex morphology, where star-convex polygons structurally cannot represent branched organelles. It beats Mask R-CNN only where dim objects fall below the detector's box-proposal threshold and therefore never receive a mask at all. And it improves on Cellpose only along the noise axis, and only through the specific, cited mechanisms above — never as a blanket claim of superiority. This honesty is itself part of the design: a flow-based method that shares Cellpose's decoder should justify its existence by the parts that differ (noise-aware backbone and loss), not by re-branding the shared parts.

**What a practitioner would do next.** To turn this blueprint into a working tool, a practitioner assembles a pixel-accurate instance-labeled set representative of the target modality; calibrates the camera constants `a` (gain) and `b` (read-noise variance) from a photon-transfer or flat-field measurement, or equivalently applies a generalized-Anscombe input transform; precomputes the flow and border-weight targets from the masks; trains `AttnResUNetFlow` under `TouchingInstanceFlowLoss`; and tunes the §6 decode hyperparameters (`τ_fg`, `smooth_sigma`, `n_iter`, `min_area`, attractor spacing) against AJI/PQ/AP@IoU on held-out data, moving each knob against the metric it controls. Warm-starting the backbone from a Cellpose-style flow prior is a reasonable way to reduce label demand while preserving the noise-aware loss and decode. The through-line — regress a geometry-encoding flow, supervise it with a noise-matched objective, and decode it into emergent instances — is what makes the pipeline both principled and modular: any single stage can be swapped (a different backbone, a Tversky foreground term, a local-maxima sink finder) without breaking the `f_x, f_y, p_fg` contract that holds it together.

## Verification notes

This document is a **design blueprint, not a benchmarked product**, and the following caveats apply to it in full.

- **Untrained, no weights.** All code uses random initialization. There is no trained checkpoint and no released generalist model; nothing here has been fit to data. The model, loss, and decoder are architecturally complete and shape-consistent, but their accuracy is unestablished.
- **The smoke tests check plumbing, not performance.** The `__main__` blocks verify head shapes, differentiability of the objective, and end-to-end tensor flow. They confirm the contract is wired correctly; they say nothing about segmentation quality, which requires training and evaluation not included here.
- **No benchmarks, no dataset validation.** No accuracy, AJI, PQ, or AP number is claimed. The metrics named in §6–§7 are *evaluation hooks* for a future results section, not reported results. Every behavioral claim is *by design* / *expected* from a stated mechanism, not measured.
- **Calibration is assumed, not performed.** The noise-model parameters `a` (gain) and `b` (read-noise variance) and the placeholder defaults (`a=1.0, b=25.0`) must be recovered per camera; the border-weight and decode hyperparameters must be tuned to the actual cell-vs-organelle morphology and spacing.
- **Scope boundaries.** 2D few-channel input only (3D z-stacks out of scope); a single integer label map cannot represent true multi-object pixel overlap (resolved here as winner-take-nearest-sink); flow-following can over/under-segment extremely elongated shapes without hyperparameter tuning.
- **Deployment asymmetry vs. baselines.** Cellpose ships generalist pretrained models usable with little or no fine-tuning; the proposed design has no released checkpoint, so realizing its noise-robustness benefits requires training on labeled data or transfer from a Cellpose-style prior.
