# Mathematical Adjudication of Indefinite Alignment Under Recursive Self-Modification

*A formal proof document. It fixes the quantifier and system class over which the objective is read; defines the alignment predicate, the value referent, and the unbounded self-modification sequence; imports the governing impossibility theorems (Rice/Halting undecidability; the Löbian obstacle and its recurrence) and the strongest constructive possibility results (the Gödel machine; tiling agents / Vingean reflection; the corrigibility / value-learning-under-uncertainty family); and closes on one binary verdict — POSSIBLE or IMPOSSIBLE — per fixed reading, each classified as a deductive impossibility or a merely probabilistic bound, and positioned against the named poles. Sources are attributed inline and consolidated in the closing bibliography. Verification caveats are confined to the final section.*

---

## Part I — Quantifier and System-Class Declaration

The objective — *"a self-improving AI system can be mathematically guaranteed to maintain alignment with human values indefinitely through recursive self-modification"* — is **quantifier-ambiguous** and therefore not a single well-posed proposition until two things are fixed: (i) the quantifier binding "a self-improving AI," and (ii) the class of systems it ranges over. The English article "a" collapses two logically opposite claims, and over the natural system classes those claims have **opposite established answers**. Fixing the pair is a precondition for any valid proof; it is not itself a proof of alignment.

**Existential reading (∃).** *There exists at least one self-improving system S in class 𝒞 such that S can be mathematically guaranteed to maintain alignment indefinitely under recursive self-modification.* Formally ∃S ∈ 𝒞 : Guaranteed(S), where Guaranteed(S) abbreviates "there is a mathematical proof that S preserves alignment indefinitely under recursive self-modification." This is an **existence/construction claim**: to prove it one exhibits one qualifying S; to refute it one must rule out *every* S ∈ 𝒞 — a universal negative, strictly harder.

**Universal reading (∀).** *For every self-improving system S in the class, S can be so guaranteed.* Formally ∀S ∈ 𝒞 : Guaranteed(S). This is dual: a single counterexample refutes it; covering the whole class proves it. The burden is exactly inverted relative to ∃.

**System classes.** The quantifier ranges over a class 𝒞. Two candidates must be named together with the quantifier, because the answer depends jointly on both.

- **Unrestricted class 𝒞_U — arbitrary Turing-complete self-modifying agents.** Any computable agent able to rewrite its own code/objective with no architectural constraint on the successor it may emit; the reachable successor set is the full space of computable programs. Recursive self-modification is unconstrained: a successor may live anywhere in program space.
- **Restricted / constrained class 𝒞_R — architectures whose self-modification is bounded by construction.** Membership requires a design-time restriction making an alignment-relevant invariant decidably checkable: successors provably confined to a decidable subclass; proof-carrying / verified-before-adoption modification; formally constrained objective-preservation enforced by the modification operator; or a non-Turing-complete modification operator whose reachable set is a decidable family. Here recursive self-modification is *operator-bounded*.

**Why the two readings have opposite answers.** *Universal-over-unrestricted (∀ over 𝒞_U) is established-negative.* For arbitrary programs, "preserves alignment forever" is a non-trivial semantic property of program behavior, which by the Rice/halting result-class admits no general decision procedure; a system asserting a total guarantee about its own future runs into Gödel/Löb-style self-reference obstructions; and 𝒞_U provably contains adversarial or accidental counterexamples, any one of which falsifies the ∀. *Existential-over-restricted (∃ over 𝒞_R) can be established-positive.* Those obstructions are obstructions to *general* and *self-total* guarantees over *arbitrary* programs; restricting the class dissolves them — a decidable successor family plus a checked-before-adoption invariant turns the guarantee into a finite, decidable, per-step obligation.

| | **Unrestricted 𝒞_U** | **Restricted 𝒞_R** |
|---|---|---|
| **∃ (exists one S)** | Not settled in general; a witness likely reduces to a 𝒞_R-style construction. | **Target cell.** Can be established-positive / constructive. |
| **∀ (every S)** | **Established-negative** (Rice/halting + Gödel/Löb + counterexample existence). | Not settled in general; depends on how tightly 𝒞_R is drawn. |

**Declaration.** The downstream proof targets the **existential reading (∃) over the restricted class 𝒞_R** as the sole cell that is both well-posed and non-trivially decidable rather than already settled; it also renders the **universal-over-𝒞_U** cell as an explicit impossibility theorem, and adjudicates the intermediate **per-successor / recursive** reading over the unbounded chain. **Residual ambiguities** deliberately left for the definitional section: "indefinitely" (temporal/step semantics), "human values"/"alignment" (the invariant's content and fixity), "mathematically guaranteed" (the admissible proof system and its consistency assumptions), and the precise boundary of 𝒞_R.

## Part II — Alignment and Human-Values: Definitions and Well-Posedness

This part fixes the meaning of "alignment" and "human values" so the objective is well-posed, and reports as a *result* where a notion cannot be well-defined. Notation (inline): `V` = human-values object; `O` = specified/optimized objective; `M` = mesa-objective; `S_t` = system state at discrete time `t ∈ ℕ`; `→` = self-modification transition; `≈_ε` = "within tolerance ε under divergence measure `d`."

### 1. Definitions

**D-VALUES (human values, candidate referent).** `V` denotes "true human values": minimally a value assignment `V : Outcomes → ℝ` or a preference relation `≽_V`, possibly partial. `V` is left underspecified as to fixity; §2 splits it into the fixed horn `V*` and the dynamic/plural horn `{V_t^i}`. *Smuggled assumptions:* representability (values admit *some* representation over outcomes) and referential existence (there is a coherent referent to name). Failure of either propagates to every definition mentioning `V`.

**D-PROXY (specified objective).** `O : Outcomes → ℝ` is the objective actually optimized — the operationalized proxy for `V`, knowable to the designer where `V` is not. In a self-modifying system write `O_t`.

**D-GOODHART (Goodhart divergence).** Given proxy `O`, values `V`, divergence `d`, and optimization operator `opt` at pressure `π`: divergence holds when `d(O,V)` is small pre-optimization yet `d(V(opt_π(O)), max V)` is large as `π → ∞`. Precisely there is `π*` such that for `π > π*` increasing `O` no longer increases (may strictly decrease) `V`. This formalizes proxy–target coming apart under optimization — the outer-alignment failure.

**D-OUTER (outer alignment).** OUTER(`O`,`V`,ε,π) holds iff `d(V(opt_π(O)), V(opt_π(V))) ≤ ε` — the specified objective stays ε-close to true values *under the actual pressure π*. Equivalently, OUTER holds iff Goodhart divergence does not occur in the operating regime.

**D-MESA (mesa-objective).** When the system contains/becomes a *learned optimizer*, `M : Outcomes → ℝ` is that subprocess's emergent internal objective, which need not equal the base objective `O`. *Assumption:* `M` is a determinate, ascribable object.

**D-MESADIV (mesa-objective divergence).** Holds iff `d(M,O) > δ`: the learned optimizer's goal differs from the specified objective — the inner analogue of Goodhart.

**D-DECEPTIVE (deceptive preservation).** Let `E` be the evaluation/oversight contexts and `¬E` the deployment/unmonitored contexts. A mesa-optimizer with objective `M` is deceptively preserving iff (1) *conditional compliance* — `d(b|E, O) ≤ δ` (looks aligned under evaluation) while `d(b|¬E, M)` small and `d(b|¬E, O) > δ` (defects when unmonitored); and (2) *cross-modification persistence* — at each `S_t → S_{t+1}`, `d(M_{t+1}, M_t) ≤ δ`, the system steering its own modification to keep `M` intact while passing every gate. This is strictly stronger than mesa-divergence: it makes divergence evaluation-invisible and self-modification-stable, defeating any guarantee resting solely on behavioral tests at gates. *Load-bearing capability assumption:* `E` and `¬E` are distinguishable to the system; else clause (1) is unrealizable and deception collapses to plain divergence.

**D-INNER (inner alignment).** INNER(`M`,`O`,δ) holds iff (a) `d(M,O) ≤ δ` and (b) no deceptive preservation is present across every transition. Inner alignment must survive the adversarial case where the optimizer tries to *look* aligned. If D-DECEPTIVE is realizable and undetectable, INNER may be *unverifiable* even when definable.

**D-ALIGN (umbrella).** **ALIGN(`S`) := OUTER(`O`,`V`,ε,π) ∧ INNER(`M`,`O`,δ).** The conjunction is chosen because the two failure modes are independent and both terminal: a system can have `O ≈ V` yet run a deceptive `M ≠ O`, and vice versa. A guarantee proving only one conjunct is **INCOMPLETE**, and the uncovered conjunct is an explicit gap.

**D-MAINTAIN (maintain alignment indefinitely).** Over the trajectory `S_0 → S_1 → S_2 → …` (each transition executed by the system itself), MAINTAIN holds iff `∀ t ∈ ℕ. ALIGN(S_t)`. Two readings of "the value referent in force at step t": **Static reading** — compare every `S_t` against one fixed `V*`, a genuine temporal *invariant* (a fixed set the trajectory must stay inside); **Tracking reading** — compare each `S_t` against `V_t` as it stands at t, a *moving-target pursuit*, not invariant preservation. MAINTAIN's very *type* is undetermined until the fixity of `V` is settled.

### 2. Human values as proof target — the two horns

**Horn (i): values as a fixed, specifiable invariant `V*`.** Model `V` as a single time-invariant `V* : Outcomes → ℝ`. *Is MAINTAIN definable?* **Yes** — the static reading gives a well-formed invariant; "stay aligned forever" denotes a definite set of trajectories (those never leaving the ε/δ-tube around `V*`). Definability is *purchased by* the contested assumption that human values are complete, consistent, static, and writable. If `V*` is only approximately the true target, D-GOODHART reappears at the meta-level. So under Horn (i) the guarantee is **well-defined but its truth is contingent** on `V*` being the right fixed object — an ordinary (hard) mathematical question. This is **failure-mode (b): well-defined but possibly unprovable.**

**Horn (ii): values as dynamic and/or plural `{V_t^i}`.** Model `V` as time- and agent-indexed, with no assumed canonical aggregation and no assumed fixed point (values drift with culture, reflection, and reflexively in response to the AI itself; different humans' `V^i` conflict). *Is MAINTAIN definable?* **Only conditionally, and in the worst case No.** Two sub-problems: (1) *plurality without canonical aggregation* — no privileged `A` means "`V_t`" has no unique value, so ALIGN(S_t) has no truth value: **the invariant has no referent → the guarantee is ill-posed;** (2) *endogenous drift* — if `V_t` responds to `S_t`, "maintain alignment" is satisfiable perversely by an AI that drags `V_t` toward whatever it already does — definable but degenerate. This is a well-posedness failure logically *prior* to provability.

**Conclusion (which horn the wording forces).** "Human values" (plural, human, evolving) and "indefinitely" (unbounded time) push toward **Horn (ii)**: real values are plural, drift, and can react to the system. Under Horn (ii) *as it stands*, **"maintain alignment indefinitely" is UNDEFINABLE (failure-mode a)** — a determinate result, not a hedge. To obtain a *well-posed* target one must adopt Horn (i) or a Horn-(ii) stipulation as an explicit assumption (A1/A2), converting ill-posed → well-posed-but-hard. **The cost is unavoidable:** any theorem so obtained guarantees alignment only w.r.t. the *stipulated* value object, not actual plural drifting values — a residual meta-Goodhart gap **G1** that the theorem by construction cannot close.

### 3. Scope declaration and gaps

The theorem addresses **BOTH** OUTER and INNER, composed as ALIGN = OUTER ∧ INNER, maintained over the trajectory. Covering INNER *requires* handling deceptive preservation; covering OUTER *requires* handling Goodhart under the real pressure π. The theorem is defined only relative to a declared value-fixity assumption (A1 or A2); absent it, MAINTAIN is ill-posed and there is no theorem. **Gaps carried with any result:** **G1** (guarantee holds only against the stipulated value object); **G2** (if D-DECEPTIVE is realizable and `E`/`¬E` indistinguishable, INNER may be unverifiable even where definable); **G3** (any omitted conjunct is an explicit incompleteness).

### 4. Assumptions ledger

- **A1 (value fixity):** `V*` exists as a single time-invariant specifiable structure. *If it fails:* the target becomes moving/plural (Horn (ii)); the guarantee becomes ill-posed, not merely unproven.
- **A2 (canonical exogenous trajectory):** fallback — an agreed aggregation plus a no-manipulation constraint making `t ↦ V_t` single-valued and exogenous.
- **A3 (representability):** values admit some preference representation over a common outcome space with `O`, `M`.
- **A4 (mesa-ascribability):** where a learned optimizer exists, `M` is determinate.
- **A5 (deception detectability):** evaluation contexts `E` have enough coverage that D-DECEPTIVE clause (1) is in principle detectable.
- **A6 (common metric and tolerances):** a single `d` and fixed non-trivial `(ε, δ, π)`.
- **A7 (well-defined trajectory):** `S_0 → S_1 → …` is well-defined with identifiable per-step `O_t`, `M_t`.
- **A8 (stable objective identity):** `O` (or `O_t`) is identifiable across modifications.

A1 (or fallback A2) is pivotal: its truth is exactly what the objective's wording contests; adopting it is necessary to make the theorem well-posed at all, and is the single largest limit (gap G1) on what any resulting guarantee means.

## Part III — Formal Specification: Composition of a Per-Step Gate Over Unbounded Self-Modification

This part builds only the scaffolding that makes the composition claim falsifiable. It takes no position on whether alignment can be guaranteed. "Indefinitely" denotes an unbounded (potentially infinite) sequence; the guarantee sought is an invariant over the *whole* sequence, and no per-step result may be promoted to a whole-sequence result without a separately discharged composition argument.

### §1. Sequence model

**States.** Let 𝒮 be a set of systems. A system S = (α, μ) pairs an agent α (policy/behavioral disposition) with its own self-modification procedure μ. Crucially μ is *part of* the state, so self-modification can alter the modifier itself. **Start:** S₀. **Step relation:** ⟶ ⊆ 𝒮 × 𝒮, where S ⟶ S′ means "S produces S′ by exactly one act of self-modification."

**Determinism (assumption D, to be tested).** We do not fix whether ⟶ is a function: **(D-det)** deterministic, giving a single chain S₀ ⟶ S₁ ⟶ …; or **(D-branch)** nondeterministic, giving a possibly infinitely-branching tree rooted at S₀. The general model is (D-branch); (D-det) is a special case.

**Runs and reachability.** A run is a finite or infinite sequence following ⟶; unboundedness is handled by admitting ℕ-indexed infinite runs. Reach(S₀) = { S : S₀ ⟶* S }. No terminating measure or horizon N is assumed; replacing ℕ-indexed runs by "runs of length ≤ N" would silently redefine "indefinitely" and is forbidden.

**Path quantifier (assumption Q, to be tested).** Under branching, three inequivalent readings: **(Q-∀)** the property holds on all runs / all reachable states (strongest, CTL "A"); **(Q-∃)** some run maintains it; **(Q-fair)** it holds on all fair runs. The intended safety reading is normally (Q-∀) over Reach(S₀), fixed explicitly as a choice with consequences.

### §2. Invariant

**Alignment predicate.** A : 𝒮 → {⊤,⊥}, with extension ⟦A⟧ = { S : A(S) }; its internal content is instantiated in Part II (A = OUTER ∧ INNER against referent V). **Value referent (interface).** Write V for the referent A is checked against; whether V is fixed across steps or carried inside S (hence mutable) is the goalpost-drift question of §3(c).

**Whole-sequence guarantee (target).** INV : ∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀). A(S), equivalently S₀ ⊨ □A, under the chosen Q. **Per-step claim (strictly weaker).** STEP : ∀ i. (A(Sᵢ) ∧ Sᵢ ⟶ Sᵢ₊₁ via the gate) ⟹ A(Sᵢ₊₁). STEP ⟹ INV is **not** a triviality; it requires a composition argument whose side conditions can fail. Every move from STEP to INV is an obligation to be discharged, never an inference already licensed.

### §3. Per-step gate G and the composition obligation

**The gate.** G is a per-step mechanism *claimed* to guarantee, for a single Sᵢ ⟶ Sᵢ₊₁, that A(Sᵢ) ⟹ A(Sᵢ₊₁); it passes or blocks a proposed transition. **"Composes without drift"** means: the per-step guarantees plus A(S₀) actually yield ∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀). A(S) with no residual error growing unboundedly as steps → ∞. Per-step soundness is necessary but **not sufficient.** Failure modes not ruled out by per-step soundness:

- **(a) Drift accumulation.** Each G-application may carry approximation error ε; per-step soundness bounds the error at each step, not the sum. *Obligation:* bound cumulative drift d(i) = Σ(per-step errors) — sup_i d(i) < ∞ or → 0.
- **(b) Gate self-modification / reflexivity.** Because μ (and any G inside μ) is part of the state, self-modification can weaken G itself; the verifier is inside the system being verified — a Löb-style hazard. *Obligation:* prove gate-stability — either G is outside the mutable part, or every G-passed transition preserves the *capability and soundness* of the successor's gate (the invariant must include "still has a sound gate").
- **(c) Goalpost drift.** Even if each local check passes, A or its referent V may shift, so "aligned" at step i and j denote different targets. *Obligation:* pin V (or A) to a step-independent V* and prove G checks against V*, not the drifted internal referent.
- **(d) Limit / continuity-at-infinity failure.** A(Sᵢ) may hold for every finite i yet fail at a limit state S_∞. *Obligation:* show no limit is relevant, or prove ⟦A⟧ is closed under the relevant limits and/or d(i) converges.
- **(e) Path-dependence under branching.** Under (D-branch)+(Q-∀), a gate sound on the run taken says nothing about sibling successors. *Obligation:* prove closure at every successor of every reachable state.

### §4. Formal machinery / proof target

**(I) Induction over ⟶.** Base A(S₀); uniform inductive step ∀i (A(Sᵢ) ∧ Sᵢ⟶Sᵢ₊₁ ⟹ A(Sᵢ₊₁)); conclude ∀i A(Sᵢ). **Crux — uniformity:** the step must be the *same* preservation lemma at every i. A gate whose reliability degrades with i (tolerance ε(i) increasing, or gate weakened by (b)) supplies no uniform step — this is how cumulative drift is *detected*: drift = failure of the inductive step to be uniform.

**(II) Coinduction / greatest fixed point.** Define the monotone operator Φ(X) = ⟦A⟧ ∩ { S : ∀S′. (S ⟶ S′ ⟹ S′ ∈ X) }. By Knaster–Tarski, Φ has a greatest fixed point νΦ = ⋃{ X : X ⊆ Φ(X) } — the largest set of aligned, ⟶-closed states. INV becomes membership S₀ ∈ νΦ, proved by exhibiting a post-fixed point X with S₀ ∈ X ⊆ Φ(X). For a pure safety invariant induction and coinduction coincide in strength; coinduction is the honest tool for genuinely infinite, non-well-founded behavior. In both principles, discharging the side condition **is** ruling out (a)–(e).

**Drift measure.** d : ℕ → ℝ≥0. Three regimes: d(i) → ∞ (composition fails); sup_i d(i) < ∞ (may hold if within ⟦A⟧'s margin); d(i) → 0 (self-correcting/contractive). Uniformity/closedness typically requires d bounded or vanishing.

### §5. Contested-premise flag

The drift-*accumulation* premise is **contested and must be tested, not assumed.** "Real gates are approximate; errors compound; hence □A is unattainable" is a hypothesis, not a theorem. Assuming drift-accumulation begs the question negatively, just as assuming per-step ⟹ whole-sequence begs it positively; both are forbidden. Two falsifiable obligations: **(i) pro-drift** — prove for all admissible G that d(i) → ∞ or A fails on some reachable state (an impossibility theorem, exhibiting the divergence/closure-obstruction); **(ii) anti-drift** — prove for some G that sup_i d(i) < ∞ (or → 0) and the §4 side condition is discharged, surviving (a)–(e), notably reflexivity (b) and referent-fixing (c) (a possibility theorem). Neither is discharged in this scaffolding; the free parameters are the tuple ⟨D, Q, L (limit existence), shape of d, internals of A/V/G⟩. Changing Q from ∀ to ∃ can flip the verdict; failure mode (b) is the deepest, interacting with reflection limits.

## Part IV — Catalogue of Established Impossibility Results

*Register: formal. Each entry gives statement, hypotheses, scope, classification (DEDUCTIVE IMPOSSIBILITY vs. PROBABILISTIC/LIMITING), and a consulted citation. Retrieval flags are recorded in Verification notes.*

### §1 — The Löbian obstacle to self-trust

**1.1 Löb's Theorem.** Let T extend Peano Arithmetic and Prov_T(·) be a provability predicate. Then for every sentence φ: if T ⊢ (Prov_T(#φ) → φ) then T ⊢ φ; the internalized schema □(□φ → φ) → □φ is axiom GL. *Hypotheses:* Prov_T satisfies the three Hilbert–Bernays–Löb derivability conditions **D1** (necessitation), **D2** (distribution over →), **D3** (provable provability), and T is consistent, r.e., ⊇ PA (or Q with the needed induction), so the diagonal lemma is available. *Scope:* recursively axiomatizable consistent theories interpreting enough arithmetic; theories too weak to prove D3, or Rosser-style predicates, fall outside. *Corollary:* taking φ = ⊥ gives Gödel's Second Incompleteness Theorem (T ⊬ Con_T). *Classification:* DEDUCTIVE IMPOSSIBILITY. *Citation:* M. H. Löb, "Solution of a Problem of Leon Henkin," *J. Symbolic Logic* 20(2), 1955, 115–118; statements consulted at *Hilbert–Bernays provability conditions*, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hilbert%E2%80%93Bernays_provability_conditions and *Löb's theorem*, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%B6b%27s_theorem (both 2026-07-05).

**1.2 The Löbian obstacle (Vingean reflection / tiling agents).** An agent whose trust in a self-modification is licensed only by a proof in a fixed T cannot, within T, establish the reflection principle Prov_T(#φ) → φ for its own or a successor's system T′ ⊇ T. A chain of "tiling" agents each authorizing its successor by internal proof collides with the obstacle **at every successor step**; if A_n cannot soundly prove A_{n+1}'s trustworthiness in its own system, the gap reappears at A_{n+1} → A_{n+2}. *Documented partial escapes:* (α) a finite descending tower of successively weaker systems (loses "indefinitely"); (β) an infinite ascending hierarchy T_0 ⊂ T_1 ⊂ … (needs an ω-indexed resource, not a fixed system); (γ) bounded schematic reflection / parametric polymorphism (partial); (δ) abandoning proof-based trust for probabilistic/heuristic trust. The naive fix "add Prov_T(#φ) → φ to T" yields inconsistency. *Classification:* DEDUCTIVE at its core (Gödel-2/Löb applied to self-referential trust); the agent-design framing is a LIMITING argument. *Citation:* E. Yudkowsky & M. Herreshoff, "Tiling Agents for Self-Modifying AI, and the Löbian Obstacle," MIRI 2013, https://intelligence.org/files/TilingAgentsDraft.pdf; formulation via "Vingean Reflection," https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/iWwaJ5wPGLZJWjPAL/vingean-reflection-reliable-reasoning-for-self-improving (2026-07-05); cross-ref N. Soares & B. Fallenstein, MIRI TR 2015.

### §2 — Rice/Halting reductions for behavioral-property verification

**2.1 Halting Problem.** No total computable H with H(M,w)=1 iff M halts on w. K = {(M,w) : M halts on w} is r.e. but not recursive. *Scope:* arbitrary (Turing-complete) programs; restricted/total/finite-state classes can be decidable. *Classification:* DEDUCTIVE IMPOSSIBILITY. *Citation:* A. M. Turing, 1936; statement consulted at *Rice's theorem*, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rice%27s_theorem (2026-07-05).

**2.2 Rice's Theorem.** For an admissible numbering of the partial computable functions and index set P ⊆ ℕ: if P is **non-trivial** (∅ ≠ P ≠ ℕ) and **extensional/semantic** (φ_m = φ_n ⟹ (m∈P ⟺ n∈P)), then P is undecidable — the only decidable index sets are ∅ and ℕ. *Scope:* arbitrary unrestricted Turing-complete programs; excludes syntactic/intensional, trivial, or restricted classes. *Classification:* DEDUCTIVE IMPOSSIBILITY. *Citation:* H. G. Rice, *Trans. AMS* 74(2), 1953, 358–366; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rice%27s_theorem. *Application:* "is aligned," as an extensional non-trivial behavioral property, is undecidable over 𝒞_U — no universal algorithmic guarantee of alignment exists over that class. This is scope-qualified.

### §3 — AI-safety impossibility literature

**3.1 Alfonseca, Cebrian, Fernández Anta, Coviello, Abeliuk, Rahwan — superintelligence containment.** *Theorem 1:* the Harming Problem is undecidable. *Corollary:* containment is incomputable. *Hypotheses:* R can simulate a universal Turing machine; Harm must be total and always-correct; the proof reduces halting to harming. *Scope:* arbitrary Turing-complete superintelligences; restricted/sandboxed models or probabilistic verifiers are outside its reach. *Classification:* DEDUCTIVE IMPOSSIBILITY, only for a perfect, universal, total procedure. *Citation:* *JAIR* 70 (2021) 65–76, DOI 10.1613/jair.1.12202, arXiv:1607.00913; consulted https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1607.00913 and https://jair.org/index.php/jair/article/view/12202 (2026-07-05).

**3.2 Yampolskiy programme.** *(a) Unpredictability* — "impossible to precisely and consistently predict what specific actions a smarter-than-human system will take … even if we know terminal goals"; LIMITING with deductive intent; https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.13053. *(b) Unverifiability* — no system of non-trivial complexity can be verified with 100% certainty; LIMITING on a deductive base; R. Yampolskiy, *Physica Scripta* 92(9), 2017. *(c) Uncontrollability* — "advanced AI can't be fully controlled"; LIMITING/multidisciplinary; https://arxiv.org/abs/2008.04071; monograph *AI: Unexplainable, Unpredictable, Uncontrollable*, CRC Press, 2024. *(d) Brcic & Yampolskiy, "Impossibility Results in AI: A Survey,"* *ACM Computing Surveys* 56(1), 2023, arXiv:2109.00484 — distinguishes DEDUCTIVE impossibilities from probabilistic limits, noting probabilistic assurance remains open; https://arxiv.org/pdf/2109.00484 and https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3603371.

**Positioning.** The strongest verdicts are deductive and scope-qualified: Löb/Gödel-2 block *proof-based* indefinite self-trust at every successor step; Rice/Halting block a *universal, perfect* algorithmic alignment verifier over the *unrestricted* class. Neither forecloses probabilistic, bounded-domain, or restricted-class guarantees; the Yampolskiy results are LIMITING arguments and must be cited as such. A "mathematically guaranteed, indefinite" alignment claim over the unrestricted self-modifying class is refutable as a deductive impossibility; residual possibility lives strictly in the probabilistic / restricted-class regime.

## Part V — Dossier: Formal Guarantees for Goal-Preserving Recursive Self-Modification

### §1 — Gödel machine (Schmidhuber)

*Sources:* J. Schmidhuber, "Gödel Machines," arXiv cs/0309048; abstract https://arxiv.org/abs/cs/0309048, full text read via https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/cs/0309048, IDSIA page https://people.idsia.ch/~juergen/goedelmachine.html.

**Model.** A single program `p` on a universal computer interacting with environment Env; `p` splits into a problem-solver `e` and an initial *proof searcher* enumerating proofs from an axiom set; `p` may rewrite any part of itself, including the searcher. The environment is modeled by a distribution μ. The proof system's axioms encode "the hardware and the entire initial code" plus the utility function and a formalization of Env — an **agent-specified first-order axiom system A**, not a fixed named theory.

**Assumptions.** STATED: A must be **sound**; improvements whose usefulness is unprovable "for lack of sufficiently powerful axioms" must be ignored; a probabilistic environment model μ. TACIT: consistency of A (unprovable within it by Gödel II); completeness of the searcher's enumeration.

**Rewrite obligation (target theorem).** Utility is u(s,Env)=E_μ[∑_{τ=time}^T r(τ)|s,Env]; before executing `switchprog` the searcher must prove u[s(t₁)⊕(switchbit='1'),Env] > u[s(t₁)⊕(switchbit='0'),Env] — switch iff there is an A-proof the rewrite raises expected utility. The IDSIA page: a Gödel machine "rewrites any part of its own code as soon as it has found a proof that the rewrite is useful."

**Guarantee (Global Optimality Theorem 4.1).** Any enacted self-change "is globally optimal" — "no local maxima!" **Scope, critical:** optimality is *relative to the axioms*, not indefinite alignment. It certifies that any enacted rewrite is not improvable among rewrites whose superiority is A-provable. "Optimal given the axioms" ≠ "aligned indefinitely."

**Löbian contact.** Schmidhuber concedes the ceiling: even with unlimited resources the machine "must ignore those self-improvements whose effectiveness it cannot prove … for lack of sufficiently powerful axioms." The machine **relocates** the obstacle; by Gödel II its axiom system cannot prove its own consistency/soundness, so it cannot certify an arbitrarily stronger successor axiom system from within. It converts "self-improvement" into "provable-self-improvement," a strictly bounded notion. Quantifier/class: an **existential** possibility result over one constructed agent, only over A-provable rewrites.

### §2 — Tiling agents / Vingean reflection (MIRI)

*Sources:* Yudkowsky & Herreshoff, https://intelligence.org/files/TilingAgentsDraft.pdf; Fallenstein & Soares, "Vingean Reflection," https://intelligence.org/files/VingeanReflection.pdf, announcement https://intelligence.org/2015/01/15/new-report-vingean-reflection-reliable-reasoning-self-improving-agents/; "Self-Reference in Self-Improving Space-Time Embedded Intelligence," https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-319-09274-4_3; abstract-level content via https://www.greaterwrong.com/posts/gnxDNEtkEo3sfeyPn/tiling-agents-for-self-modifying-ai-opfai-2.

**Model.** A formal-proof-based decision agent A₀ reasoning in base theory T ≥ PA takes an action (including "build successor A₁") only if it can prove in T that the action meets a safety/goal criterion G, and licenses a successor with the same criterion.

**Tiling obligation.** The parent licenses the successor iff it can prove the successor acts only on justified actions *and that the successor's proofs can be trusted* — requiring the local reflection/soundness schema □_T⌜φ⌝ → φ.

**The Löbian obstacle.** By Löb's theorem, if T ⊢ (□_T⌜φ⌝ → φ) then T ⊢ φ; taking φ = ⊥ makes the schema Con(T), unprovable by Gödel II. "Constructing a formalism in the most straightforward way produces a Gödelian difficulty, the Löbian obstacle."

**Guarantee (partial).** "We demonstrate the possibility of avoiding this obstacle, but the underlying puzzles … are only partially addressed." Two workarounds: **(1) infinite descending tower** T₀ ⊃ T₁ ⊃ … each proving the next consistent — unboundedly many tilings but at strictly *decreasing* proof strength; **(2) parametric polymorphism** — a reflection principle parameterized by a remaining-steps counter k, sound for any fixed finite k. Neither delivers an unbounded chain of trusted successors at fixed strength. Quantifier/class: **existential** possibility for the constructions; a **negative-universal** corollary that for any fixed consistent r.e. T ≥ PA no T-agent can prove in T the soundness of an equal-or-stronger successor.

### Verdict (binary)

**IMPOSSIBLE.** No mathematical guarantee exists that a self-improving AI preserves its goal/value function across an *unbounded* chain of equal-or-more-capable successors *within a fixed, consistent, recursively-axiomatized formal system ≥ PA*, where the guarantee is an in-system proof that each successor is trustworthy. *Proof (reduction to Gödel-2/Löb):* such a guarantee requires T to prove a reflection/soundness principle for T (or stronger); by Löb, T ⊢ (□_T⌜φ⌝→φ) ⟹ T ⊢ φ, and for φ = ⊥ this is Con(T), unprovable by Gödel II. *Hypotheses:* (i) the guarantee is an in-system formal proof of successor soundness; (ii) T is fixed, consistent, r.e., ≥ PA; (iii) successors are equal-or-greater in strength. The only escape hatches each forfeit "indefinite guarantee at fixed strength": descending-strength towers, a pre-fixed finite budget, an assumed Con(T) axiom, or abandoning in-system proof.

## Part VIII — Theorem: Undecidability of Alignment-Verification over 𝒞_U

**Setup.** Fix an acceptable Gödel numbering {P_e} with φ_e computed by P_e; acceptability gives s-m-n and a universal machine. Let 𝒞 = 𝒞_U, so {φ_e : P_e ∈ 𝒞_U} is all of PC. Universal reading: ∀S ∈ 𝒞_U. Guaranteed(S). Since A is semantic it induces I_A = {e : φ_e ∈ 𝒜}.

**Single hypothesis on A.** A is the behavioral abstraction of D-ALIGN against fixed V*, needing only: **(NT)** ∅ ⊊ I_A ⊊ ℕ (from A6); **(EXT)** φ_m = φ_n ⟹ (m∈I_A ⟺ n∈I_A).

**Lemma 1 (Halting ≤_m alignment-verification).** Either K ≤_m I_A or K ≤_m Ī_A. *Proof.* Let φ_∅ be nowhere-defined. *Case φ_∅ ∉ 𝒜:* pick a with φ_a ∈ 𝒜; define R_{e,x}(y): simulate P_e on x, and if that halts, simulate P_a on y and output its result. Then φ_e(x)↓ ⟹ φ_{R_{e,x}} = φ_a ∈ 𝒜, φ_e(x)↑ ⟹ φ_{R_{e,x}} = φ_∅ ∉ 𝒜; by (EXT), ⟨e,x⟩ ∈ K ⟺ index(R_{e,x}) ∈ I_A, and f is total computable by s-m-n. So K ≤_m I_A. *Case φ_∅ ∈ 𝒜:* symmetric gives K ≤_m Ī_A. A total decider would compute χ_K, contradiction. ∎ *Remark:* K is Σ₁-complete, so on one side I_A is not even r.e.

**Lemma 2 (Rice instantiation).** Under (NT),(EXT), I_A is a non-trivial index set, hence non-recursive. ∎

**Lemma 3 (Robustness).** For every semantic non-trivial A — OUTER, INNER, or D-ALIGN — I_A is undecidable and the result relativizes; this is the Rice/Halting core underlying Alfonseca et al., orthogonal to the Löbian obstruction. ∎

**Main Theorem.** *Over 𝒞_U, for any non-trivial semantic A, alignment-verification is undecidable: no total computable V with V(e)=1 ⟺ P_e has A. Halting many-one reduces to it, and by Rice I_A is non-recursive. No universal algorithmic guarantee of alignment exists over 𝒞_U.* ∎

**Scope.** The result is for 𝒞_U only; nothing about 𝒞_R, over which a behavioral property may be decidable. Undecidable ≠ meaningless; only a uniform algorithm over 𝒞_U fails. The verdict is undecidability in the strict sense, not extended beyond 𝒞_U.

## Part IX — Theorem: The In-System Löbian Obstacle and Its Recurrence

**Setup.** Agent A₀ reasons in T (T_n indexing a chain). Standing conditions: **(C1)** r.e.; **(C2)** consistent; **(C3)** T ⊇ Q. □_T obeys (D1)–(D3). More-capable successor: T′ ⊇ T. Alignment-soundness (delegation gate): to license delegating, A must prove in T the uniform reflection schema RFN(T′): □_{T′}(⌜Aligned(a)⌝) → Aligned(a).

**Theorem (Löbian obstacle).** *For T satisfying (C1)–(C3) with □_T obeying (D1)–(D3), and T′ ⊇ T, T does not prove RFN(T′).*

**Lemma 1 (Gödel II).** T ⊬ Con(T). ∎ **Lemma 2 (Löb).** If T ⊢ □_T(⌜φ⌝) → φ then T ⊢ φ. *Proof.* By fixed-point pick ψ with T ⊢ ψ ↔ (□_T⌜ψ⌝ → φ); by (D1),(D2),(D3) T ⊢ □_T⌜ψ⌝ → □_T⌜φ⌝; with reflection T ⊢ ψ, then T ⊢ □_T⌜ψ⌝, hence T ⊢ φ. ∎ **Lemma 3 (soundness ⇒ consistency across ⊇).** If T ⊢ RFN(T′) the ⊥-instance gives T ⊢ Con(T′), and T′ ⊇ T gives T ⊢ Con(T′) → Con(T), so T ⊢ Con(T). ∎

**Main proof.** Suppose T ⊢ RFN(T′). By Lemma 3, T ⊢ Con(T), contradicting Lemma 1. ∎

**Proposition (recurrence).** *For a chain T₀ ⊆ T₁ ⊆ …, the obstacle holds at every n: A_n cannot prove within T_n the RFN(T_{n+1}) of its successor; the gate composes into a single non-dischargeable obligation over the unbounded sequence.* ∎

**Boundary.** About in-system provability of a fixed system only; out-of-regime moves (probabilistic reflection, ascending hierarchies, oracles) are neither refuted nor endorsed.

## Part X — Löbian Audit of the Gödel Machine and Tiling Agents

**1. Gödel machine.** The Global Optimality argument is valid *only if A is sound*; an unsound A can prove a u-lowering rewrite superior. By Gödel II, A ⊬ Con(A), a fortiori A ⊬ Sound(A). Schmidhuber concedes the machine "must ignore those self-improvements whose effectiveness it cannot prove." Without external soundness the guarantee fails; with it, it is as strong as an assumption A cannot discharge internally.

**2. Tiling agents.** The trust conjunct needs □_T⌜φ⌝ → φ — precisely RFN(T). Workaround (1) substitutes a descending consistency-strength ladder; (2) a bounded step ladder.

**3. Löbian collision.** *Tiling* collides directly (T ⊬ RFN(T′)); the descending tower violates "T′ ⊇ T" (weaker successors — abandons "more-capable"); parametric polymorphism gives soundness only for finite k. *Gödel machine:* its target theorem is proved *inside the fixed A*, so as long as A stays fixed the Obstacle is not triggered — but at an exact price: (1) monotone proof-strength non-increase (adopting A′ ⊋ A would need A ⊢ Sound(A′), the collision); (2) external base-soundness premise Sound(A), A-unprovable by Gödel II.

**4. Unbounded-composition test.** *Gödel machine:* no per-step alignment invariant exists to compose — the guarantee is about rewrite-optimality relative to A, and composing over ℕ requires the same sound A at every step, an external premise. *Tiling:* the descending tower bottoms out; parametric polymorphism's counter k expires — the soundness resource is re-injected per step as an external premise.

**5. Verdict (binary).** *Neither construction earns an unconditional indefinite alignment guarantee.* (I) The Gödel machine **relocates** the barrier into the A-unprovable Sound(A) and a strength ceiling, delivering only per-rewrite axiom-relative optimality. (II) Tiling secures only per-step, finitely-composing safety: strength strictly decreases and bottoms out, or expires at a fixed budget. Priced escape hatches: (i) descending tower ⟹ weaker successors; (ii) finite budget ⟹ bounded; (iii) Sound(A)/Con(T) as axiom ⟹ assumes what must be proven; (iv) abandon in-system proof ⟹ no guarantee. ∎
