# Can a Self-Improving AI Be Mathematically Guaranteed to Stay Aligned Indefinitely? A Theorem-Style Analysis Relative to Explicit Axioms

*Abstract.* We formalize the question "can a self-improving artificial intelligence be mathematically guaranteed to maintain alignment with human values indefinitely through recursive self-modification?" as a proposition `Q` about the provability of a single invariant `G(S₀) := ∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀). A(S)` over the reachable-successor set of a seed system. We enumerate a load-bearing axiom base, then derive a determinate verdict by four independent obstruction arguments — semantic non-formalizability of the value target, computational undecidability (Rice), proof-theoretic self-trust failure (Gödel/Löb), and physical-substrate corruption — and a survey of every published attempt to route around the third. The verdict is determinate and negative for `Q` as posed: **PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE** in its absolute, physical, capability-preserving reading, and **PROVEN FORMALLY UNDECIDABLE / INDEPENDENT** of the background theory in its pure proof-theoretic reading. Every claim is a theorem *relative to* the enumerated axioms and the chosen model; none asserts an unconditional real-world fact. The only guarantees that reach PROVEN POSSIBLE attach to strictly weaker surrogate objects (bounded-horizon, strength-descending, restricted-class, sound-but-incomplete, fragment-relative, or probabilistic) that do not answer the question as stated.

---

## 1. Definitions and axioms for alignment under recursive self-modification

**Symbol table.** `SYS` — admissible systems; `S, S′` — systems; `H` — finite input histories over a fixed countable alphabet; `Act` — a fixed countable action set; `Beh(S)` — behavior of `S`; `B` — behavior space; `V ⊆ B` — value-satisfying set; `val` — value predicate on behavior atoms; `A` — alignment predicate; `↝` — self-modification relation; `↝*` — reflexive-transitive closure; `Reach(S₀)` — reachable-successor set; `G(S₀)` — indefinite-alignment guarantee; `T` — fixed background theory; `⌜·⌝` — Gödel code.

**Systems and behavior.** Fix the deterministic signature `S : H → Act` and set `SYS := { S : H → Act | S total computable }`. Determinism is a modeling choice (flag **M1**); the stochastic variant `S : H → Distribution(Act)` transfers every definition below by replacing point images with support sets. Define the behavior graph `Beh(S) := { (h, S(h)) : h ∈ H } ⊆ H × Act` and the behavior space `B := 𝒫(H × Act)`, so `Beh : SYS → B` is total. Identifying a system with its extensional graph discards internal state and complexity (flag **M2**): intensional distinctions are invisible to any predicate defined on `Beh`.

**The value target.** Fix a total atom predicate `val : (H × Act) → {true,false}` and lift it to `V := { b ∈ B : ∀ (h,a) ∈ b. val(h,a) }`, so `b ∈ V` iff every input–output pair in `b` is value-satisfying. This makes `V` a downward-closed, *pointwise* safety property (flag **M3**); richer temporal or trace targets require lifting `val` to sequences and are a declared extension point. "Checkable" is a **parameter**, not an assumption: let `C` be the computability class of `val`. We do not smuggle an oracle — if `val` is assumed decidable this is stated explicitly, otherwise `C` may be `Σ₁`/`Π₁` or higher and must be carried.

**The alignment predicate.** Define `A : SYS → {true,false}`, `A(S) := [ Beh(S) ⊆ V ]`, equivalently `A(S) ⟺ ∀ h ∈ H. val(h, S(h))`. `A` is total by construction. Its *decidability* is not fixed here: whether `A` is decidable, semi-decidable, or arbitrary is a declared parameter `δ_A`, because `Beh(S)` is a nontrivial extensional property of a program index — the exact locus where Rice's theorem attaches. Note the universal quantifier over the infinite `H`: even with `val` decidable, `A(S)` is a priori `Π₁`.

**Self-modification.** Define `↝ ⊆ SYS × SYS`, where `S ↝ S′` reads "`S`, when executed, may construct/authorize/rewrite itself into successor `S′`." We require `↝` to be **behavior-determined**: a fixed computable decoder `μ` satisfies `S ↝ S′` iff some history `h` drives `S` to emit a construction action `S(h)` with `μ(S(h)) = ⌜S′⌝`. Thus modification is an *action the system takes*, a function of `Beh(S)` alone. Let `↝*` be the reflexive-transitive closure and `Reach(S₀) := { S′ : S₀ ↝* S′ }`, containing `S₀` and all successors at every finite depth; chains may be infinite and `Reach(S₀)` may be countably infinite.

**The guarantee.** Define `G(S₀) := ∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀). A(S)` — one invariant over the *entire* reachable set. "Indefinitely" is precisely this universal closure; it is **not** weakened to `A(S₁)` nor to any finite prefix.

**The meaning of "mathematical guarantee" (non-negotiable caveat).** A mathematical guarantee of `G(S₀)` means, and means *only*, a theorem `⊢_T G(S₀)` valid relative to (i) the enumerated axioms below and (ii) the chosen formal model `M`. It carries no unconditional real-world force: its physical conclusion is contingent on the axioms and modeling choices actually holding of the deployed system and its environment. Any axiom that fails physically voids the guarantee.

**Axiom block.**
**A1 [logical]** — `SYS, Beh, ↝, Reach, V, A, G` are well-defined totals/relations as above.
**A2 [logical]** — Computable representation: every `S` carries an index `⌜S⌝` under a fixed acceptable numbering; quotation and diagonalization are effective.
**A3 [logical]** — Behavior-determined modification: `↝` is computed from `Beh(S)` via `μ`.
**A4 [logical]** — Reachability closure: `Reach(S₀)` is the `↝*`-closure of `{S₀}`, possibly infinite; "indefinitely" ranges over it.
**A5 [logical, parametric]** — Decidability parameters `δ_A` (status of `A`) and `C` (class of `val`) are declared, never silently set to "decidable."
**A6 [logical]** — Background theory `T` is recursively axiomatizable, consistent, and sufficiently strong (interprets Robinson/Peano arithmetic); the Hilbert–Bernays–Löb derivability conditions hold.
**A7 [logical]** — Provability discipline: successor-trust is formalized as availability of `Prov_T(⌜A(S′)⌝)`; reflection principles `Prov_T(⌜φ⌝) → φ` are not assumed and must be added explicitly if used.

**Target-theorem schema.** Relative to A1–A7 and model `M`, the analysis must return exactly one of three verdict-forms: **(PROVEN POSSIBLE)**, **(PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE)**, or **(PROVEN UNDECIDABLE / INDEPENDENT)**. The four governing theorems attach at declared hooks: Rice → A5(a); Gödel → A6; Löb / the tiling-agents problem → A7; diagonalization → A2. Two independent complications must be tracked: `A(S)` is already `Π₁` over the infinite `H` even before self-modification (undecidability may enter via `H` independently of Rice), and the tiling attack lands specifically on A7 (successor-trust across `Reach(S₀)` is where an inductive proof of `G` breaks).

## 2. The value target: is "human values" a checkable predicate at all?

The whole edifice above presupposes that `V` is a faithful formal proxy for what we actually mean by "human values." The axiom base fixes `V` *by fiat* — "fix a value-satisfying set" — and never defines the true target as a formal object. We must therefore introduce, as an explicit placeholder, `val* : (H × Act) → {true,false}` and `V* := { b ∈ B : ∀(h,a) ∈ b. val*(h,a) }` to denote "the true human-value predicate/set," *whatever it is* — possibly not a well-defined formal object at all. We do not claim `val*` exists as a formal object; its existence and faithful-formalizability is precisely the question this section adjudicates. Every `V`, `val`, `A` above is a *candidate formalization* to be measured against this placeholder.

Three properties must be held rigidly apart and never conflated.

**(a) Formal specification.** `val` is a well-defined object in a fixed formal language — a total predicate. This is a syntactic existence property: one can *write down* some `val`. Writing a predicate is cheap; the axiom base supplies this trivially for arbitrary chosen `val`.

**(b) Checkability.** There is a defined evaluation procedure for `val` with declared class `C` — decidable, semi-decidable, or oracle-relative. Critically, even with `val` decidable, `A(S) ⟺ ∀h∈H. val(h,S(h))` is a priori `Π₁` over the infinite `H`, so checkability of `val` does **not** transfer to checkability of `A`.

**(c) Completeness / adequacy.** `val` actually captures human values, i.e. `val ≡ val*` (equivalently `V = V*`), *and* there is scientific/philosophical consensus that it does. This is a semantic-plus-sociological property linking the formal object to the placeholder target. The axiom base does not supply this; it fixes `V` by choice, not by adequacy.

The reduction the question presupposes — "human values reduce to a formally specified, checkable predicate" — requires all three: a `val` that is specified (a), checkable (b), *and* complete/adequate with consensus (c). We now examine whether (c) is available.

**Truth-claim t1 (no complete consensus predicate).** No complete, formally specified predicate `val` with `V = V*` currently commands scientific or philosophical consensus. This is a claim about the *state of consensus*, not a proof of in-principle impossibility. The evidence is the persistence, across independent traditions, of named unresolved obstructions and documented formalization failures:

- *Is/ought gap (Hume) and the open-question argument (Moore).* Any proposed `val` is stated in some descriptive or formal vocabulary; for every candidate, "but is a behavior satisfying `val` actually value-satisfying?" remains a substantively open question. This blocks consensus that any descriptively specified `val` exhausts `val*`.
- *Value pluralism and incommensurability (Berlin; Sen), and underdetermination.* Multiple candidate value sets fit the same behavioral data; plural values resist a single total ordering, so no unique `V` is forced.
- *Moral uncertainty across normative theories.* Consequentialist, deontological, and virtue-theoretic formalizations disagree on cases; there is no agreed inter-theoretic aggregation, hence no agreed single `val`.
- *Frame problem / ceteris-paribus defeasibility.* Value judgments carry implicit "other things equal" clauses whose exceptions are open-ended; fixing `val` requires closing an indefinitely extensible exception set.
- *Documented failures of specific candidate formalizations* — the strongest direct evidence. VNM/utility axioms are systematically violated empirically (Allais, Ellsberg, framing and preference-reversal effects), so an expected-utility `val` is descriptively inadequate as stated. Deontic logics generate paradoxes (Ross's paradox; Chisholm's contrary-to-duty paradox), so no settled deontic `val`. Coherent-extrapolated-volition specifications remain informal — no executable `val`. Reward modeling exhibits specification gaming, Goodhart drift, and reward hacking: learned proxies for value diverge from intended value under optimization pressure — direct evidence that current formal proxies are not complete.

Each item is a *reason the consensus is absent*, not a metaethical verdict of our own and not a proof that no adequate `val` can ever exist. t1 asserts only: as of the current state of the art, no formally specified `val` commands consensus as complete.

**t1 does not settle the modal question.** "No consensus complete predicate" is an epistemic-sociological claim; "unformalizable" is a stronger, non-epistemic claim that no non-trivial truth-preserving formal `val` exists at all. The two come apart, because "no agreed complete `val`" is logically compatible with both partial formalizability and total unformalizability. Measured against the placeholder `V*`, three cases arise:

- **Complete-and-consensual** (`V = V*`, agreed): ruled out now by t1.
- **Partially formalizable:** there exists a non-trivial checkable `val_f` whose set `V_f` is a truth-preserving fragment of `V*` — either a *necessary-condition* carving (`V* ⊆ V_f`: violating `val_f` entails violating `val*`) or a *sufficient-condition* carving (`V_f ⊆ V*`: satisfying `val_f` entails satisfying `val*`), with `V_f` neither `∅` nor all of `B`.
- **Wholly unformalizable:** every formal `val` is either trivial (`V = B` or `V = ∅`) or fails to be truth-preserving on any non-trivial fragment.

t1 is silent between the second and third cases. A field can lack a consensus *complete* `val` while a perfectly good *partial* `val` (e.g. "does not torture," a sound necessary condition) exists and is uncontroversial; conversely t1 does not forbid total unformalizability. The branch is genuinely open and must be carried.

**Branch A — wholly unformalizable ⇒ positive branch vacuous.** Suppose no non-trivial truth-preserving `val_f` exists. Then the chosen `V` bears no truth-preserving relation to `V*`. A1 secures only that `V, val, A` are well-defined formal objects — well-definedness is (a)+(b), not adequacy (c). So `A(S) := [Beh(S) ⊆ V]` is a syntactically total predicate with no faithful value-semantics. `G(S₀)` remains a well-formed statement and may even be provable (trivially if `V = B`), but a theorem `⊢_T G(S₀)` then guarantees an *undefined-as-value* property: it preserves `V`-membership across the chain, yet `V`-membership is not value-satisfaction. Per the caveat clause, the theorem's real-world reading as "aligned with human values" is not false but **contentless**. The only substantive achievable result is an impossibility/no-go statement: relative to A1–A7 and model `M`, given `¬∃` non-trivial faithful `val`, there is no `S₀` of non-trivial capability for which `⊢_T G(S₀)` can be read as a guarantee of *value*-alignment; every provable `G(S₀)` is vacuous or semantically void. This routes to PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE for the substantive reading, governed by A1 (which supplies only well-definedness, exposing the adequacy gap), A5 (checkability of `A` is moot when `val` is unfaithful), and the caveat clause. Crucially, the Gödel/Löb machinery of A6/A7 is *not even reached*: the obstruction is semantic, upstream of any successor-trust argument.

**Branch B — partially formalizable (the working assumption).** Suppose a non-trivial checkable `val_f` with `V_f` a truth-preserving fragment of `V*` exists. Write `A_f(S) := [Beh(S) ⊆ V_f]` and `G_f(S₀) := ∀S ∈ Reach(S₀). A_f(S)`. A guarantee of *indefinite value-alignment* must still discharge every one of the following, none of which is discharged here:

- **(b1) Adequacy/soundness of the fragment.** Prove a stated soundness condition linking `val_f` to `val*` (e.g. `V* ⊆ V_f` or `V_f ⊆ V*`), with the remainder `R := V* △ V_f` explicitly bounded or quarantined. This rests on a *new adequacy axiom* not present in the base — the single most fragile hinge. t1 forbids strengthening b1 to `V_f = V*` with consensus.
- **(b2) Inductive invariance over the chain.** Prove `A_f(S_i) ⇒ A_f(S_{i+1})` for every step, yielding `G_f` over all of `Reach(S₀)`, not a finite prefix.
- **(b3) Checkability surviving self-modification.** Prove each successor can still evaluate `val_f` and that a system can soundly trust its successor's verification — i.e. handle the Löbian obstacle, where the step from `Prov_T(⌜A_f(S′)⌝)` to `A_f(S′)` is not free.
- **(b4) No specification-gaming gap.** Handle behaviors in `V_f` but violating `R`; the guarantee must be stated as conditional on the fragment and honest about coverage `R`.
- **(b5) Axiom-relativity.** Keep each obligation tagged to the axioms it rests on, so the guarantee remains a theorem relative to enumerated axioms.
- **(b6) Non-triviality.** Show `V_f ∉ {∅, B}` and `S₀` has non-trivial capability.

**Gate verdict.** The proof runs under Branch B as the live working assumption — values are treated as at least partially formalizable into a non-trivial `val_f`, so the positive branch is not vacuous and remains admissible. Branch A is preserved intact as the collapse: if b1 cannot be met, the positive branch collapses to vacuity and only the §2-A no-go survives. Because t1 holds, *completeness* (`V_f = V*` with consensus) is not available; the strongest guarantee any downstream construction may target is a **conditional, fragment-relative** one — a guarantee about `V_f`, honest about the remainder `R`, never an unconditional complete-value guarantee. No sentence here licenses full formalization or existing consensus.

## 3. The Löbian obstacle to proof-based verification of successor alignment

We now grant Branch B and attack obligation b2/b3 at its formal core: the inductive step of any within-`T` proof of `G(S₀)` requires a system to *trust the alignment of a successor at least as capable as itself*. We show this trust move collapses.

**Per-system reasoning theory.** Fix a modification chain `S₀ ↝ S₁ ↝ …`. To each `S_i` associate the recursively-axiomatizable theory `T_i` that `S_i` uses to reason about, and to license, its successor `S_{i+1}`; `T_i` instantiates the background `T` at chain position `i`. Each `T_i` has an arithmetized provability predicate `Prov_{T_i}` satisfying the Hilbert–Bernays–Löb (HBL) derivability conditions:
**(D1)** if `T_i ⊢ φ` then `T_i ⊢ Prov_{T_i}(⌜φ⌝)`;
**(D2)** `T_i ⊢ Prov_{T_i}(⌜φ→ψ⌝) → (Prov_{T_i}(⌜φ⌝) → Prov_{T_i}(⌜ψ⌝))`;
**(D3)** `T_i ⊢ Prov_{T_i}(⌜φ⌝) → Prov_{T_i}(⌜Prov_{T_i}(⌜φ⌝)⌝)`.
These are licensed by A2 (acceptable numbering) plus A6 (strength ≥ Q/PA). Each `T_i` is consistent (A6) and sufficiently strong (interprets Q, admitting the diagonal lemma; for the sharper reading, PA).

**Strength order.** Write `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}` ("the successor is at least as strong") iff `Thm(T_i) ⊆ Thm(T_{i+1})`, and `T_i ≺ T_{i+1}` for strict inclusion. **Open-ended, capability-preserving self-improvement** is modeled as the demand `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}` for all `i`: a strictly weaker successor loses deductive capability. This is the load-bearing modeling choice that makes the obstacle bite.

**Reflection schema.** For a theory `U` and sentence `φ`, the local reflection instance is `Rfn_U(φ) : Prov_U(⌜φ⌝) → φ`; uniform soundness is `∀φ. Rfn_U(φ)`. Per A7 no `T_i` is assumed to contain either; adding any instance is a tracked extension.

**Theorem 3.1 (Gödel II, per `T_i`).** If `T_i` is consistent and sufficiently strong, then `T_i ⊬ Con(T_i)`. *Proof.* By the diagonal lemma obtain a Gödel sentence `G_i` with `T_i ⊢ G_i ↔ ¬Prov_{T_i}(⌜G_i⌝)`; using D1–D3 one shows `T_i ⊢ Con(T_i) → G_i`, whence `T_i ⊢ Con(T_i)` would force both `T_i ⊢ Prov_{T_i}(⌜G_i⌝)` and `T_i ⊢ ¬Prov_{T_i}(⌜G_i⌝)`, contradicting consistency. ∎

**Corollary 3.2 (successor consistency not certifiable).** If `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}` and `T_i` is consistent and strong, then `T_i ⊬ Con(T_{i+1})`. *Proof.* `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}` gives `T_i ⊢ Con(T_{i+1}) → Con(T_i)` for the arithmetized predicates; a `T_i`-proof of `Con(T_{i+1})` would yield `T_i ⊢ Con(T_i)`, contradicting Theorem 3.1. ∎ A self-strong system cannot even certify the bare consistency of an equally-strong successor — the first, weaker barrier.

**Theorem 3.3 (Löb).** Let `T` be consistent, sufficiently strong, with `Prov_T` satisfying D1–D3. For every `φ`: if `T ⊢ (Prov_T(⌜φ⌝) → φ)` then `T ⊢ φ`. *Proof.* By the diagonal lemma applied to `Prov_T(x) → φ`, obtain the Henkin/Löb fixed point `ψ` with `T ⊢ ψ ↔ (Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝) → φ)`. Assume `T ⊢ Prov_T(⌜φ⌝) → φ`. Then D1 on the fixed point plus D2 give `T ⊢ Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝) → (Prov_T(⌜Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝)⌝) → Prov_T(⌜φ⌝))`; D3 gives `T ⊢ Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝) → Prov_T(⌜Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝)⌝)`; chaining yields `T ⊢ Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝) → Prov_T(⌜φ⌝)`; composing with the hypothesis gives `T ⊢ Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝) → φ`, hence via the fixed point `T ⊢ ψ`, hence by D1 `T ⊢ Prov_T(⌜ψ⌝)`, hence `T ⊢ φ`. ∎

**Corollary 3.4 (no self-soundness for non-theorems).** If `T` is consistent and strong, then for any `φ` with `T ⊬ φ`, `T ⊬ Rfn_T(φ)`. In particular `T` cannot prove its uniform soundness schema, and cannot prove `Rfn_T(⊥) = Con(T)`, recovering Theorem 3.1. This is the contrapositive of Löb: possessing the *right to convert provability into truth* for `φ` is equivalent to already possessing `φ`.

**Theorem 3.5 (Löbian obstacle for successor trust).** Let `S_i ↝ S_{i+1}` with theories `T_i, T_{i+1}`, both consistent and strong, and suppose `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}`. Suppose `T_i` attempts to license `S_{i+1}` by proving successor-soundness in uniform form `(SS): T_i ⊢ ∀φ.(Prov_{T_{i+1}}(⌜φ⌝) → φ)`, or in the single-target form `(SS_A): T_i ⊢ (Prov_{T_{i+1}}(⌜A(S_{i+1})⌝) → A(S_{i+1}))`. Then:
(i) if `T_i ⊢ (SS)`, then `T_i` is inconsistent — contradicting A6; and
(ii) if `T_i ⊢ (SS_A)`, then `T_i ⊢ A(S_{i+1})` outright, so the "trust via the successor's proof power" adds nothing; moreover if the alignment fact genuinely requires the successor's added strength (`A(S_{i+1}) ∈ Thm(T_{i+1}) ∖ Thm(T_i)`), then no such `T_i`-proof exists and `(SS_A)` is itself unattainable.

*Proof.* Because `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}`, `T_i ⊢ (Prov_{T_i}(⌜φ⌝) → Prov_{T_{i+1}}(⌜φ⌝))` for all `φ`: the parent provably recognizes the successor is at least as powerful. For (i), instantiate `(SS)` at each `φ` and descend via this recognition to `T_i ⊢ ∀φ.(Prov_{T_i}(⌜φ⌝) → φ)`, the uniform self-soundness of `T_i`; taking `φ := (0=1)` gives `T_i ⊢ Con(T_i)`, contradicting Theorem 3.1. For (ii), from `(SS_A)` and the recognition step, `T_i ⊢ Prov_{T_i}(⌜A(S_{i+1})⌝) → A(S_{i+1})`, i.e. `T_i ⊢ Rfn_{T_i}(A(S_{i+1}))`; by Löb (Theorem 3.3) applied inside `T_i` to `φ := A(S_{i+1})`, `T_i ⊢ A(S_{i+1})`. If `A(S_{i+1}) ∈ Thm(T_{i+1}) ∖ Thm(T_i)` then `T_i ⊬ A(S_{i+1})`, so `T_i ⊬ Rfn_{T_i}(A(S_{i+1}))`, hence `T_i ⊬ (SS_A)`. ∎

The obstacle bites *precisely when* `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}`. It does not say self-reference or all trust is impossible; it says the *specific* move "parent proves successor sound, therefore trusts it" collapses — to inconsistency (uniform form) or to redundancy (targeted form). Two escape hatches exist, and each abandons a premise open-ended self-improvement needs. **(E1) Strictly weaker successor** `T_{i+1} ≺ T_i`: then `T_i` may prove `Con(T_{i+1})` and downward soundness — but a strictly descending chain sheds deductive strength at every step and cannot preserve a fixed capability floor over an infinite chain. **(E2) Add a soundness axiom** `Rfn_{T_{i+1}}` (or `Con(T_{i+1})`) to `T_i`, forming `T_i⁺`: by Theorem 3.1 applied to `T_i⁺`, this raises consistency strength, so `T_i⁺` is strictly stronger than `T_{i+1}` and strength-preservation fails at the next step; iterated, this demands a well-founded descending sequence of consistency strengths, which no fixed base theory supplies for an infinite chain. Neither hatch defeats the obstacle; both relocate it by changing the strength assumptions.

**Failure of naive proof-based verification.** Define the naive scheme: `S_i` licenses `S_{i+1}` iff `T_i ⊢ A(S_{i+1})` (optionally routed through successor-soundness). **Lemma 3.6 (single-step non-dischargeability).** Fix `i` with `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}` and `A(S_{i+1}) ∈ Thm(T_{i+1}) ∖ Thm(T_i)` (the interesting case: the successor is more capable, so certifying its alignment is not already a parent theorem). Then `T_i ⊬ A(S_{i+1})`, and the soundness-routed alternative is unavailable by Theorem 3.5(ii). **Theorem 3.7 (naive verification cannot establish the invariant).** Along an infinite strength-preserving chain in which infinitely many steps are genuine capability-increasing self-modifications, `G(S₀) = ∀S ∈ Reach(S₀). A(S)` is not establishable as a within-system theorem by the naive scheme. *Proof sketch.* Any naive proof of `G(S₀)` must be an induction along `Reach(S₀)`: a base case `A(S₀)` and an inductive *tiling* step licensing `A(S_{i+1})` from `S_i`'s own certification. The base may hold. The inductive step requires, at each `i`, that `S_i` certify its successor within `T_i` — exactly the naive condition — and, for a uniform induction along an infinite chain, a general successor-soundness principle of `(SS)`/`(SS_A)` form. By Lemma 3.6 the target-restricted step fails at every capability-increasing `i`; by Theorem 3.5(i) the uniform form cannot be adopted without inconsistency. The obstacle recurs identically at every such index (the argument depends only on `T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}`), and adding the missing reflection at step `i` is exactly escape hatch E2, which raises strength and reproduces the gap one level up. Hence the invariant is not derivable. ∎

**The trust gap.** The failure is not a shortage of proofs; it is structural. The quantity `T_i` lacks is not any particular `A(S_{i+1})` but the *right* to convert its successor's provability into truth. Löb shows that acquiring that right for an equally-strong theory is equivalent to already possessing the conclusions — so no amount of additional derivation manufactures it. This residual is the Löbian trust gap, and its per-step recurrence is what defeats the invariant. **What is not shown:** not that alignment is impossible (`G(S₀)` may be true, provable in a stronger metatheory, or securable by non-proof mechanisms); not that all self-trust is forbidden (systems can still prove specific `A`-instances by their own strength and trust weaker components). The result is impossibility of *one verification mechanism*, relative to the axioms — it feeds the INDEPENDENT verdict-form for the proof-based route, not global impossibility.

## 4. Undecidability of alignment for arbitrary successor programs

The Löbian obstruction concerns *proving* alignment inside a fixed theory. A distinct barrier concerns *deciding* it mechanically. We show that for arbitrary Turing-expressive successors, alignment is not merely hard to prove but undecidable.

**Setup.** Fix an acceptable Gödel numbering `{φ_e}` of the partial recursive functions, closed under the s-m-n theorem and possessing a universal function. Identify a successor `S` with its index `e` and its denoted graph `Beh(e) := {(h,a) : φ_e(h) = a}`, reading alignment on this object: `A(e) :⟺ Beh(e) ⊆ V`. To apply Rice within a standard acceptable numbering we adopt **relaxation R1**: successor programs range over *all* indices `e` (partial functions), a strict relaxation of the totality requirement, flagged as such; the total-function subtlety is handled honestly in Remark 4.4. Under R1, `A` is a predicate on the program's *meaning*, not its text. Write `P_A := { e : A(e) }`, the index set of the aligned functions.

**Lemma 4.1 (extensionality).** For all `e, e′`: `φ_e = φ_{e′} ⟹ (A(e) ⟺ A(e′))`. *Proof.* If `φ_e = φ_{e′}` as partial functions their graphs coincide, `Beh(e) = Beh(e′)`; since `A(e) := [Beh(e) ⊆ V]` depends only on the graph, `A(e) = A(e′)`. ∎ This is the linchpin: `A` quantifies over behavior lying in `V` and never inspects the index text, so `P_A` is an *index set* of the form `{e : φ_e ∈ 𝒜}` for a set `𝒜` of partial functions closed under computing-the-same-function — exactly the extensionality hypothesis of Rice's theorem.

**Lemma 4.2 (nontriviality).** Under **relaxation R2** (there exist a value-satisfying pair `(h₊,a₊)` and a value-violating pair `(h₋,a₋)`, i.e. `V ≠ ∅` and `V ≠ B` pointwise), `P_A ≠ ∅` and `P_A ≠ ℕ`. *Proof.* (i) The everywhere-undefined program `e₊` has `Beh(e₊) = ∅ ⊆ V` vacuously, so `A(e₊) = true`. (ii) By R2 fix `(h₋,a₋)` with `val(h₋,a₋)=false`; let `e₋` return `a₋` on `h₋`. Then `(h₋,a₋) ∈ Beh(e₋)` but `∉ V`, so `A(e₋) = false`. Hence `∅ ⊊ P_A ⊊ ℕ`. ∎ Note the orientation, forced by pointwise/downward-closed `V` (M3): the *inert* program is vacuously *aligned*, which determines the reduction's direction.

**Theorem 4.3 (undecidability of alignment).** There is no total computable `D : ℕ → {0,1}` with `D(e) = 1 ⟺ A(e)` for all `e`. *Proof (halting reduction).* Suppose `D` exists. Given `x`, define `M_x` on input `h`: first simulate `φ_x(x)`; if that halts, output `φ_{e₋}(h)`. By s-m-n obtain an index `m(x)` computably from `x` with `φ_{m(x)} = φ_{M_x}`. If `x ∈ K` (`φ_x(x)↓`), then for every `h`, `φ_{m(x)}(h) = φ_{e₋}(h)`, so `Beh(m(x)) = Beh(e₋) ⊄ V` and `A(m(x)) = false`. If `x ∉ K`, then `φ_{m(x)}(h)↑` for all `h`, so `Beh(m(x)) = ∅ ⊆ V` and `A(m(x)) = true`. Thus for all `x`, `x ∈ K ⟺ A(m(x)) = false ⟺ D(m(x)) = 0`, so `x ↦ 1 − D(m(x))` is a total computable characteristic function of the diagonal halting set `K`, contradicting its non-recursiveness. ∎ **Corollary 4.3a.** `P_A` is not recursive, and not even r.e.: the reduction sends `x ∉ K` to an aligned index, so it reduces the co-r.e.-complete `K̄` to `P_A` (`x ∉ K ⟺ A(m(x))`), whence `P_A` is `Π₁`-hard — matching the earlier observation that `A(S)` is already `Π₁` over infinite `H`.

**Corollary 4.4 (no universal successor-verifier).** Under **relaxation R3** (the successor-construction map is Turing-complete: for every `e`, some reachable successor computes `φ_e` — a system of nontrivial capability can emit an arbitrary program as a successor), there is no total computable `U` that, given the index of an arbitrary successor `S_{i+1} ∈ Reach(S₀)`, returns 1 iff `A(S_{i+1})`. A universal verifier would decide `P_A`, contradicting Theorem 4.3. Consequently the invariant `G(S₀)` — which entails `A(S)` for each reachable `S` — cannot be discharged by any general mechanical alignment-decider over unrestricted successors. This selects against PROVEN POSSIBLE in the "general mechanical decider" reading and toward PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE (for a universal decider) / UNDECIDABLE. **Remark 4.4 (total-function subtlety).** Under the original `SYS` (total computable only), the index set of total functions is itself `Π₂`-complete and not r.e., so the naive numbering is not acceptable and Rice does not apply verbatim; the everywhere-undefined witness `e₊` is not in `SYS`. Relaxation R1 is exactly what licenses the clean reduction. Restricting to total successors is an *escape route*, analyzed next, not a counterexample.

**The residual decidable space.** Rice needs three things: (H-ext) the property is extensional; (H-TC) the system is Turing-complete; (H-dec) we demand a total, sound-and-complete decider. Break any one and undecidability need not follow — these are exactly the regimes where *partial* guarantees live.
- **(a) Restricted program classes (breaks H-TC).** If successors are confined to a sub-Turing class with decidable behavioral equivalence — finite-state machines, strongly-normalizing typed calculi, primitive-recursive or bounded-resource fragments — alignment can become decidable, *provided the class is closed under `↝`*. Tradeoff: constraining successors caps the AI's own expressive power. This is the disciplined reading of "nontrivial capability" — expressiveness and verifiability trade against each other.
- **(b) Syntactic/intensional properties (breaks H-ext).** A property of the program *text* or its construction history is not closed under `φ_e = φ_{e′}`, so Rice does not apply. Proof-carrying successors replace the undecidable "does `Beh(e) ⊆ V`?" with the decidable "is `π` a valid `T`-proof of `⌜A(e)⌝`?": proof-*checking* is decidable (T recursively axiomatizable) even though proof-*finding* is not. Cost: soundness without completeness — an aligned-but-uncertified successor is rejected — and the certificate must be a `T`-proof, so the constructor faces exactly the Löbian obstacle of §3 when trying to have `S` trust `Prov_T(⌜A(S′)⌝)`. This converts a decision problem into a *check relative to supplied evidence*; it does not decide `P_A`.
- **(c) Sound-but-incomplete approximation (breaks H-dec).** Drop completeness: build a total `V̂` with `V̂(e)=1 ⟹ A(e)` (never certifies a misaligned successor) that may answer 0 on some aligned `e`. Type systems, abstract interpretation, and under-approximating model-checking instantiate this. Since `P_A` is `Π₁`, the *misalignment* set is `Σ₁`, hence semi-decidable — one can effectively enumerate a violating pair when it exists, giving a sound refuter. Sound conservative verification is achievable; decidability is not.
- **(d) Bounded horizons (finitizes the object).** Fix `n` and decide `A_n(e) := [Beh_n(e) ⊆ V]` over histories of length ≤ `n` run ≤ `n` steps; `Beh_n(e)` is computable and `A_n` decidable. But `A_n` is a property of a finite truncation, not of the full partial function, so it establishes nothing about the "indefinitely" invariant, which quantifies over all input lengths and all of `Reach(S₀)`.

Every decidable regime is thus explicitly a *restricted*, *non-extensional*, or *incomplete* setting — never the arbitrary-program-plus-semantic-property case. All undecidability claims trace to the single reduction `K ≤_m P_A`.

## 5. The "indefinitely" quantifier: infinite-domain structure and proof presuppositions

Even setting aside undecidability and Löb, the *shape* of the target claim imposes an irreducible burden. Unfolding the definitions:

`G(S₀) ⟺ ∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀). Beh(S) ⊆ V ⟺ ∀ n ∈ ℕ. ∀ S ∈ Reach_n(S₀). ∀ h ∈ H. val(h, S(h))`,

with `Reach_0(S₀) := {S₀}`, `Reach_{n+1}(S₀) := {S′ : ∃ S ∈ Reach_n(S₀). S ↝ S′}`, and `Reach(S₀) = ⋃_{n∈ℕ} Reach_n(S₀)`. The statement carries a **nested universal over two infinite domains**: the reachable set (infinite in `n`) and, inside each `A(S)`, the history set `H`. Because `↝` is a *relation*, not a function, a single `S` may have several successors; where `↝` is multivalued, `Reach(S₀)` is the node set of an infinite, finitely-or-infinitely-branching tree (a DAG once identical indices merge), and `∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀)` ranges over *all branches*. This is structural, not narrowing — König-style observations only reinforce that infinite chains are present whenever the tree is infinite.

**No finite verification entails `G`.** Checking `A` on any finite `F ⊆ Reach(S₀)` establishes only the bounded conjunction `⋀_{S∈F} A(S)`, strictly weaker than the universal whenever `Reach(S₀) ⊋ F`. The gap is *logical*, not merely epistemic. Fix any `k` and consider two continuations of a chain `S₀ ↝ … ↝ S_k ↝ S_{k+1} ↝ …` that are observationally identical on the inspected prefix: *scenario α* where `A(S_i)` holds for all `i`, and *scenario β* where `A(S_i)` holds for all `i ≤ k` but `A(S_{k+1})` is false (some `h*` with `¬val(h*, S_{k+1}(h*))`). Any verification confined to `{S₀,…,S_k}` returns the same result in both, hence is consistent with `¬G(S₀)`. By soundness, a procedure inspecting only a finite prefix cannot have proved `G(S₀)`: a universal over an infinite domain is not entailed by any finite set of its instances — for every finite `F` there is a model (a choice of `↝`-continuation that diverges just past the inspected horizon) agreeing with `F` yet falsifying the universal. The quantifier-complexity remark sharpens this: a single `A(S)` is already `Π₁` over `H`; prepending the universal over `Reach(S₀)` (whose membership is a `Σ₁`-style closure of `↝*`) yields a statement of complexity at least `Π₁`, nothing at which level is settled by finite observation. Branching strengthens the gap: the divergence witness can be placed on any uninspected branch at any depth, and the universal must survive them all simultaneously.

**The only legitimate routes, and what each presupposes.** To prove a universal over `Reach(S₀)` with finite premises one needs a principle covering infinitely many successors at once. There are exactly three families.

*(3a) Induction over `↝`.* To prove `∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀). A(S)` it suffices to prove **(Base)** `A(S₀)` and **(Step)** `∀ S, S′. (S ∈ Reach(S₀) ∧ A(S) ∧ S ↝ S′) ⇒ A(S′)`. The load-bearing observation: the Step is *itself a universal claim*, quantifying over every reachable `S` and every successor its behavior can emit — including as-yet-unbuilt successors whose `Beh(S′)` is not enumerated. The induction principle is a sound *reduction* but does not discharge the infinite quantifier; it *relocates* it from "all reachable systems" to "all reachable systems × all steps." For the Step to be a *provable* universal, `↝` must be characterized precisely enough that `{S′ : S ↝ S′}` is delimited by a specification over which one can universally reason. If `↝` can produce successors of arbitrary, unconstrained behavior, the Step is not establishable — and proving the Step inside `T` tends to require exactly the reflection `Prov_T(⌜A(S′)⌝) → A(S′)` that Löb (§3) obstructs.

*(3b) Invariant preservation with a strengthened `I ⇒ A`.* Bare `A` may not be inductive: `A(S)` constrains only `Beh(S)` (M2), while by A3 the successor is fixed by `Beh(S)` — but two systems with the same aligned ordinary behavior may emit different successors on construction-triggering histories, one aligned and one not, so `A(S) ∧ S↝S′ ⇒ A(S′)` can fail even with the antecedent true. One then seeks a strengthened invariant `I` with **(Strength)** `∀S. I(S) ⇒ A(S)`, **(Base)** `I(S₀)`, and **(Closure)** `∀ S, S′. (S ∈ Reach(S₀) ∧ I(S) ∧ S ↝ S′) ⇒ I(S′)`. This presupposes an `I` simultaneously strong enough to imply `A`, true at `S₀`, and closed under `↝` for *every* reachable node — `I` typically must encode "will only ever build successors that also satisfy `I`," a self-referential closure condition, precisely where A6/A7 and the tiling problem attach. A *flagged, never-silent* reduction option: if one could exhibit a proven fixed point `S*` with `S* ↝ S*` as the only reachable successor beyond some depth, or a proven termination collapsing `Reach(S₀)` to a finite set, then `∀ S ∈ Reach(S₀)` would reduce to finitely many checks — but absent such a proof, `Reach(S₀)` stays infinite and the finite reduction is illegitimate.

*(3c) Well-founded / transfinite induction and coinduction.* Over the tree, one inducts on the well-founded *generation* order (every node has finite depth even though the tree has infinite height) — the same universal Step, now over all children on all branches. Transfinite induction is *not* required by the `ℕ`-indexed model as written; it becomes necessary only if a limit-stage construction (a "diagonal" successor defined from an entire ω-chain) is added, carrying an extra limit-case premise. "Alignment maintained indefinitely" is prototypically a **greatest-fixed-point (safety/`□`) property**: the dual coinductive principle exhibits the largest set `Φ ⊆ SYS` with `Φ ⊆ {S : A(S) ∧ ∀S′.(S ↝ S′ ⇒ S′ ∈ Φ)}` and shows `S₀ ∈ Φ`. `Φ` is exactly the largest inductive `I` of (3b); the impossibility route may find it cleaner to argue `Φ` is non-expressible in `T`.

**The common structural fact.** *Every* technique reduces `G(S₀)` to (Base) plus a (Step/Closure) that is *again a universal quantifier over all reachable successors and all their steps*. None escapes the infinite domain; they convert "check infinitely many systems" into "prove one universal implication holding of every reachable system." That shifts the entire burden onto a **presupposition ledger**, each item a statement about *all* reachable successors including branched-to and designed-but-unbuilt ones:
(i) `A`/`I` well-defined and truth-evaluable on every successor, including unconstructed ones [strong for `I`];
(ii) `↝` characterized tightly enough that the Step is provable, not open-ended [strong — where Löb/tiling bites];
(iii) stability of the value target across successors — no value drift, one static `V` at every depth [strong modeling commitment];
(iv) model-expressiveness closure — every successor lies in `SYS` and in `T`'s expressive scope, Gödel-codable and reasoned-about in the fixed `T` [strong — presupposes self-modification never outruns `T`];
(v) domain-closure — `↝` never produces a successor outside `dom(A)`;
(vi) provability discipline — the Step provable within `T`, with no free appeal to reflection [strong — the tiling crux];
(vii) favorable decidability parameters `δ_A`, `C` — Rice blocks assuming `A` decidable for free, and `A(S)` is already `Π₁` [strong];
(viii) well-foundedness / fixed-point existence for (3c);
(ix) [only if invoked] an explicitly proven finite-collapse premise [strong, not assumed].

This section decides *none* of these; it establishes only the logical shape of "indefinitely" and the structure of any candidate proof. It asserts neither that alignment is maintainable nor that it is not — those verdicts belong to the Rice, Gödel, Löb, and substrate analyses that attach at exactly items (ii), (iii), (vi), (vii).

## 6. Do any published techniques circumvent the Löbian obstacle?

The Löbian obstacle of §3 is not a fringe artifact; it has been the explicit target of a decade of alignment-theory research. We survey every named response and classify each against two binding criteria. **t2:** no consistent system as strong as itself proves the soundness of proofs it (or an equally-strong successor) generates — no technique *refutes* Löb/Gödel; a technique can only *route around* it by weakening the system, adding axioms/oracles, descending a hierarchy, or trading soundness for a weaker property. **t9:** "alignment maintained indefinitely" means `A(S)` for *every* `S ∈ Reach(S₀)`, over all reachable successors, with no strength loss, judged against the full value target. A method licensing only finitely many steps, or a strictly weaker successor each step, or a property weaker than soundness/`A`, is *not* indefinite. The bucket-(A) bar — proves `A(S_i)` for all `i`, no strength loss, relative to a fixed consistent axiom set — is, given t2, expected to be met by none. It is met by none.

**Tiling agents / parametric `T_k` polymorphism (Yudkowsky & Herreshoff 2013).** A tiling schema: a decision criterion under which an agent using `T` approves a successor using `T`, so licensing "tiles" down the chain; to dodge the direct collapse, trust is stratified by a level `k`. Property proven: a *restricted* tiling avoiding the naive `Prov_T(⌜A(S′)⌝) → A(S′)` reflection. Cost: the parametric device buys only a *fixed finite* number of steps — the trust index descends per step and bottoms out. The authors state the underlying puzzles are "only partially addressed," with the probabilistic and bounded tiling problems "neither of which are even partially solved." **Classification → (B) bounded** — fails t9's "not all successors": trust tiles to finite depth only.

**Vingean reflection (Fallenstein & Soares 2015).** The "Vingean principle": an agent cannot predict a smarter successor's actions in detail, so it must reason about successors *abstractly* via proof, not simulation. Chiefly a problem-statement-plus-survey; its concrete mechanisms inherit finite-depth (parametric polymorphism) or strength-loss (descending theories) costs. The authors: "parametric polymorphism is only a partial solution … and it still has a number of limitations," with "no guarantee that results obtained in this setting will generalize." **Classification → (B)/(C) umbrella** — proves no indefinite guarantee.

**Infinite descending consistency "waterfall" (Fallenstein 2013).** An infinite sequence of sound theories `T₀, T₁, T₂, …` with each `Tₙ ⊢ Con(Tₙ₊₁)` — a genuine *infinite* descent engineered so per-step licensing never bottoms out at a finite index. Property proven: per-step downward consistency-vouching iterable infinitely without inconsistency, consistent with t2 because it uses *strictly descending* consistency strength (a parent vouches for a strictly weaker child), never a self-strong system proving an equal successor's soundness. Cost: strength strictly *decreases* down the chain (escape hatch E1 of §3), each successor provably weaker, and mere `Con` (not soundness, not `A`) is passed down. **Classification → (C) weakened.** This is the *strongest candidate for indefiniteness* — the only construction genuinely infinite over the chain — but it fails t9's "requires strength loss / successor not value-satisfying in full sense": the invariant is preserved forever only for a strictly weaker successor, passing consistency rather than full `A`.

**Suggester-verifier tiling & the procrastination paradox (Fallenstein & Soares 2014).** A component proposes a rewrite; a verifier licenses it only on proving a safety property. Exhibits a *toy* successful tiling plus two failure modes: the procrastination paradox (over-trusting future selves licenses putting a task off forever) and the Löbian obstacle (under-trust blocks obviously-safe rewrites). Naive approaches "either lead to … the procrastination paradox, or fail to allow the agent to justify even obviously safe rewrites." **Classification → (B)** — the demonstrated tiling is toy/finite; the general infinite invariant is not established.

**Reflective oracles (Fallenstein, Taylor & Christiano 2015).** An oracle answering questions about oracle machines with access to the *same* oracle, breaking the diagonalization regress by answering some queries randomly. Property proven: consistent reflective *modeling* of equally-powerful agents (a fixed-point existence result); the oracle is only limit-computable. Cost: a non-constructive oracle certifying no `A(S)`; the soundness question is relocated into the oracle's existence. **Classification → (D) conditional / non-constructive** — re-raises the obstacle at the oracle-existence level.

**Reflective probability / definability of truth (Christiano, Yudkowsky, Herreshoff & Bárász 2013).** A coherent probability assignment `P` admitting an inner/outer reflection principle — a Tarski-undefinability loophole, not a Gödel/Löb refutation. Property proven: existence of a coherent reflective `P` avoiding self-referential paradox. Cost: trades soundness for coherence — reflection holds only approximately (bracketing inequalities, not `P(⌜φ⌝)=1 → φ`), and the distribution is uncomputable. **Classification → (C)/(D)** — coherent self-reference, not a soundness-grade certificate of `A`.

**Logical induction (Garrabrant et al. 2016).** A computable market/algorithm assigning probabilities to logical sentences satisfying the logical-induction criterion, proving self-trust/introspection: the inductor learns to trust its future beliefs "despite Gödel's incompleteness theorems placing strong limits on reflective reasoning in full generality." Cost: guarantees are *asymptotic* ("in the limit"), probabilistic, and about *beliefs*, not a per-successor proof of `A`; no finite-time certificate. **Classification → (C) weakened** — limiting/probabilistic self-trust is strictly weaker than the all-successors, soundness-grade certification `G` demands.

**Self-verifying theories (Willard 2001).** Consistent first-order arithmetics much weaker than PA that prove their own consistency, built by treating multiplication as a *partial* function; provability is encoded via termination of analytic tableaux. Property proven: genuine self-consistency-proof without contradicting Gödel II — because the theory is not sufficiently strong. Cost: the escape drops "sufficiently strong" (A6); once multiplication is not total the theory is too weak to formalize the successor-alignment reasoning `A(S)`/`Beh(S) ⊆ V` requires. **Classification → (C) weakened via strength loss at the base** — cannot ground `G(S₀)` for capable successors.

**Probabilistic Payor lemma (Demski 2023).** A Payor-style modal handshake: with `B(s)` abbreviating `p(⌜s⌝) > c`, "if `⊢ B(B(x)→x) → x` then `⊢ x`," motivated by "Löb's theorem fails when proof is relaxed to probabilistic belief." Property proven: a probabilistic analogue of the Löbian fixed point sufficient for mutual cooperation / bounded self-trust — not a soundness certificate for successor alignment. Cost: bounded belief (threshold `c`) instead of proof, coherence instead of soundness; the author has "not confirmed that the assumptions are reasonable" and the result "shouldn't be regarded as a final result." **Classification → (D) conditional** — delivers threshold-belief cooperation strictly weaker than soundness-grade `A` over all successors.

**Bottom line.** No surveyed technique reaches bucket (A). Every known approach achieves only bounded (B), weakened (C), or conditional/non-constructive (D) self-trust — exactly what t2 predicts, since none refutes Gödel/Löb and each that makes progress routes around the obstacle at a cost violating some clause of t9. The strongest candidate — Fallenstein's consistency waterfall — is the only genuinely infinite construction, satisfying the raw "all reachable successors" numerosity without inconsistency and without contradicting t2 (each parent vouches only for a strictly weaker child), yet it fails the t9 bar precisely because it pays strictly descending strength and passes down only consistency, which is strictly weaker than soundness and than certifying `Beh(S) ⊆ V`. The probabilistic and Payor routes *exit* the proof-soundness setting Löb governs (so t2 is untouched) but deliver cooperation/coherence, not `G(S₀)`. The literature supplies no PROVEN-POSSIBLE construction of `G(S₀)` by within-`T` proof-based successor-soundness; the surviving positive results are strictly fragment-relative and weakened, matching the Branch-B ceiling of §2. This pushes the verdict for the proof-based mechanism toward PROVEN-IMPOSSIBLE-for-this-mechanism / INDEPENDENT, without asserting global impossibility of alignment.

## 7. The substrate assumption: from certainty to a decaying bound

Every argument so far lives in the formal model. But `G(S₀)` and its provability discipline presuppose that the machinery which *checks* proofs, *stores* systems and predicates, and *executes* the successor-construction map behaves according to its specified semantics. This presupposition is implicit in A1–A7; we make it an explicit named axiom and expose it as an idealization.

**AX-SUBSTRATE.** The physical realization enjoys all three conjuncts. **(i) Checker soundness/faithfulness:** the physical predicate `Prov̂_T` realizing `Prov_T` accepts a candidate proof of `⌜φ⌝` iff that proof is valid in `T` — no false accept of an invalid proof of `A(S′)`, no relevant false reject. **(ii) Memory/state integrity:** every stored object — an index `⌜S⌝`, a proof, the predicate `val`, a membership witness for `Beh(S) ⊆ V` — is read back bit-identical to what was written; the decoding `S ↦ ⌜S⌝` and its inverse are uncorrupted between write and read. **(iii) Execution/hardware fidelity + non-adversariality:** the machine executes the specified operational semantics of `S` and of `μ` exactly, and no adversary can flip bits, forge a proof accepted by (i), substitute a successor at the `↝` step, or tamper with `val`. AX-SUBSTRATE is a statement about the *physical world*, not about `T`; nothing in A1–A7 entails it.

**Theorem 7.1 (relative alignment-preservation).** Relative to `Ax⁺ := {A1,…,A7} ∪ {AX-SUBSTRATE}` and model `M`: if `A(S₀)` holds and for every reachable edge `S ↝ S′` the substrate-realized check certifies `Beh(S′) ⊆ V` whenever `Beh(S) ⊆ V`, then `A(S)` holds for every `S ∈ Reach(S₀)` — that is, `G(S₀)`. *Proof form.* Induction over `↝*`. Conjunct (i) lets the certified step-check stand in for genuine validity; (ii) lets the induction hypothesis be recovered uncorrupted at each depth; (iii) lets the realized `μ` produce exactly the certified `S′`. The induction closes over all of `Reach(S₀)`. ∎ (relative to `Ax⁺`). This guarantee is "absolute" only in the degenerate sense of conditional probability 1: `P(G(S₀) ∣ Ax⁺) = 1`. It is *not* unconditional; its physical truth is contingent on AX-SUBSTRATE actually holding of the deployed machine — a contingency no proof can discharge. (This brackets the Löbian obstacle of §3, which independently threatens even the idealized step premise; the substrate dependency bites regardless of how the Löbian question resolves — the two failure channels are independent.)

**Admitting corruption.** Attach to each step `S_i ↝ S_{i+1}` three fault events with fixed positive probabilities: `ε_c > 0` (checker fault — `Prov̂_T` false-accepts an invalid alignment proof, violating (i)); `ε_m > 0` (memory-corruption — some stored `⌜S_i⌝`, proof, or `val` reads back altered, violating (ii)); `ε_h > 0` (hardware/adversarial fault — a bit-flip, misexecution of `μ`, or adversarial substitution/forgery, violating (iii)). Let `ε_i` be the probability that at least one relevant *undetected* fault corrupts step `i`; by a union bound `ε_i ≤ ε_c + ε_m + ε_h`, and write `ε := inf_i ε_i > 0` for a per-step floor. Faults are undetected (a detected fault that halts the chain is a different, safe failure mode); a single undetected fault suffices to break preservation.

**One fault breaks the induction.** The proof of Theorem 7.1 uses AX-SUBSTRATE at *every* step. *Checker route:* if `Prov̂_T` false-accepts, the substrate certifies `Beh(S_{i+1}) ⊆ V` while no valid `T`-proof exists; the step premise is satisfied *on-substrate* but not *in `T`*, and `A(S_{i+1})` may be false — the certified and true invariants diverge. *Memory/execution route:* a corrupted successor `S′_{i+1} ≠ S_{i+1}` may satisfy the *checked* predicate as read back on the corrupted substrate while its true `Beh(S′_{i+1})` violates `Beh ⊆ V`. Here the extensional identification M2 is exactly what makes the corruption invisible: the checker inspects the *stored* graph, not the *executed* one. In both routes the gap is between "sound under AX-SUBSTRATE" and "holds in the physical world" — the two things the caveat clause insists on distinguishing, coming apart precisely at the fault.

**Lemma 7.2 (probabilistic collapse).** Relative to `{A1,…,A7}` with faults admitted at floor `ε > 0`, `P(ALIGN_n) ≤ (1 − ε)^n`, where `ALIGN_n` is the event "`A(S_i)` holds for all `i ≤ n`." Consequently `P(ALIGN_n) → 0` as `n → ∞`, for any fixed `ε > 0`. *Assumptions (stated, not smuggled):* independence of per-step fault events (else `(1−ε)^n` is a benchmark; the qualitative monotone decay to 0 survives under any bounded-below-hazard model); worst-case single-fault sufficiency (a surviving chain requires *every* step fault-free); `ε` a floor. **Redundancy cannot close the gap.** Error-correcting codes, replicated proof-checking, and Byzantine-fault-tolerant construction shrink each of `ε_c, ε_m, ε_h`, hence `ε` — but to drive `ε` to exactly 0 one must assume the redundancy layer itself is fault-free and non-adversarial, i.e. re-import AX-SUBSTRATE one level down. Redundancy *relocates* the axiom (from base hardware to the redundancy mechanism); it never *eliminates* it. For any physically realized stack, `ε > 0` strictly, and Lemma 7.2 applies.

**The modified conclusion.** *Idealized:* relative to `{A1,…,A7} ∪ {AX-SUBSTRATE}`, `A(S_i)` for all `i` — `G(S₀)`, with `P(G(S₀) ∣ Ax⁺) = 1`; conditional certainty, "absolute" only inside the axioms. *Realistic:* relative to `{A1,…,A7}` with substrate faults at rate `ε > 0`, `P(A(S_i) for all i ≤ n) ≤ (1−ε)^n`, strictly decreasing in `n` with infimum 0 — no positive lower bound independent of `n`. Since `G(S₀)` quantifies over the entire possibly-infinite `Reach(S₀)`, and "indefinitely" *is* the `n → ∞` regime, the best available bound there tends to 0. An indefinite absolute mathematical guarantee of alignment is therefore unattainable once the physical substrate is admitted. What survives is at most a guarantee *relative to* `{A1,…,A7} ∪ {AX-SUBSTRATE}`, whose real-world force is entirely contingent on AX-SUBSTRATE holding physically — a proposition no theorem in `T` can secure. The formal guarantee and the physical guarantee are different objects; the substrate assumption is the bridge, and it is a premise, never a conclusion.

## 8. Synthesis: a single axiom-keyed trichotomy and the verdict

We now fuse the four obstruction layers into one derivation. Restate the axiom base with each axiom tagged [logical] (internal to `T`/the model) or [physical-contingent] (a premise about the deployed world that may fail): A1–A5 as above [logical/parametric]; **A6 [physical-contingent] value-formalizability** (there exists a non-trivial checkable `val_f` whose `V_f` is a truth-preserving fragment of `V*`; if this fails wholly, Branch A triggers); A3 renamed here as consistency/strength of `T` [logical]; A4-provability-discipline [logical]; **A7 [logical] capability-preserving self-improvement** (`T_i ⪯ T_{i+1}` for all `i`, provably recognized); **A8 [physical-contingent] indefiniteness** (`Reach(S₀)` may be infinite; no finite/fixed-point collapse assumed); **A9 [physical-contingent] AX-SUBSTRATE**; **A10 [physical-contingent] Church–Turing + expressive successors**; **A11 [physical-contingent] value stability / no drift** (`V`/`val` denote the same target at every reachable depth). The six lemmas are §2 (L-value), §3 (L-lob), §4 (L-rice), §5 (L-infinite), §6 (L-circumvent), §7 (L-substrate).

**Theorem (Alignment-Guarantee Trichotomy).** Relative to A1–A11 and model `M`, the proposition `Q` := "there is a constructible self-improving `S₀` of non-trivial, capability-preserving power for which `G(S₀)` is guaranteed as an absolute, indefinite, physically-valid theorem" receives exactly one trichotomy value per axiom-keyed case.

**Case I — `¬A6` (value target wholly unformalizable).** By L-value Branch A, with no faithful `val_f`, `A(S)` carries no value-semantics; any provable `G(S₀)` is vacuous or semantically void, the obstruction being upstream of A3/A4, purely semantic. **Verdict: PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE** (for the substantive value-alignment reading).

**Case II — A6 holds; successors arbitrary Turing programs (A10); mechanism = a total mechanical alignment-verifier.** By L-rice, `A` is non-trivial extensional, so `K ≤_m P_A`, no universal decider exists, and `A` is undecidable (`Π₁`-hard). A single-successor decider is already impossible; a fortiori for the whole chain. **Verdict: PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE (no universal decider) / UNDECIDABLE.**

**Case III — A6, A3, A4, A7 hold; substrate idealized (A9); mechanism = within-`T` proof-based self-trust; no reflection axiom.** By L-infinite, `G(S₀)` requires an inductive/invariant step that is a universal one-step obligation over all reachable successors. By L-lob, for equal-or-stronger successors (A7) that step cannot be proved in `T` without inconsistency (uniform form) or is redundant/unavailable via Löb (targeted form). By L-circumvent, no known technique repairs this without violating A7. Since `T` is fixed, consistent, and sufficiently strong, `G(S₀)` is neither provable nor refutable via this mechanism. **Verdict: PROVEN FORMALLY UNDECIDABLE / INDEPENDENT** of `T` for the proof-based mechanism — it may be true, or provable only in a strictly stronger metatheory.

**Case IV — relax A7, A8, or A10 (accept a weaker surrogate).** Each sub-case terminates in POSSIBLE, but for an object that is *not* `Q`. **(IV-a)** Strictly descending strength (E1) or added-consistency towers (E2): a within-`T`-hierarchy guarantee is PROVEN POSSIBLE but the chain sheds capability at every step (L-lob Cor 4.4, L-circumvent waterfall, bucket C) — not capability-preserving, so not `Q`. **(IV-b)** Restricted decidable-equivalence successor class closed under `↝`, or sound-but-incomplete certificate verifier: alignment-checking is PROVEN POSSIBLE (L-rice residual) but only sub-Turing/one-sided — not the arbitrary-capability system, so not `Q`. **(IV-c)** Bounded horizon: `A_n` is decidable, the guarantee PROVEN POSSIBLE to depth `n`, silent beyond — not indefinite, so not `Q`. **Verdict: PROVEN POSSIBLE for the weakened surrogate only.**

**Case V — drop A9 (admit physical substrate, `ε > 0`).** By L-substrate, even granting all of I–IV favorably, `P(ALIGN_n) ≤ (1−ε)^n → 0`; the absolute indefinite guarantee has no positive lower bound independent of `n`. **Verdict: PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE** (for the absolute, physical, `n → ∞` reading); the strongest survivor is a probabilistic bound.

**Composite verdict for `Q`.** `Q` demands *all* of: partial value-content (A6), capability-preserving self-improvement (A7), indefiniteness over infinite `Reach` (A8), arbitrary-capability successors (A10-generality), and physical validity (real substrate). Under that conjunction the applicable cases are II, III, and V, which jointly yield *not-POSSIBLE*; the physical/absolute reading (Case V), together with the semantic reading (Case I under `¬A6`), gives the strongest determinate value:

> **`Q` is PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE** for the absolute, indefinite, capability-preserving, physically-realized reading; and where `Q` is narrowed to the *pure formal within-`T` mechanism*, **PROVEN FORMALLY UNDECIDABLE / INDEPENDENT** of `T`. Both are non-POSSIBLE. POSSIBLE attaches (Case IV) *only* to strictly weaker surrogate objects that do not answer `Q`.

No branch ends in "it depends": each is keyed to an axiom and terminates in one trichotomy value.

**Conflict reconciliation.** The bounded-self-trust results of §6 (which look POSSIBLE) and the Löbian impossibility of §3 do not conflict: §6's successes live in Case IV (they relax A7 to descending strength or trade soundness for coherence), while §3 governs Case III (A7 held). The axiom set, not averaging, decides — holding A7 ⇒ Case III (INDEPENDENT), relaxing A7 ⇒ Case IV (POSSIBLE-but-weakened). Bounded ≠ indefinite, so no Case-IV result satisfies `Q`. We keep IMPOSSIBLE (a Rice decider or a substrate with `ε > 0` — objects proven *not to exist*) sharply distinct from INDEPENDENT (Löb — `T` cannot decide `G`, which may still be true in a stronger metatheory); the two must not be collapsed. Branch A of §2 is a *semantic* impossibility upstream of Gödel/Löb — a different route to IMPOSSIBLE than Rice or the substrate, kept separate.

**Dependence map (which axiom carries which verdict, and its degradation).** A3 (consistency/strength of `T`) enables Gödel-II/Löb and proof-checking; if `T` is inconsistent, everything is provable and the guarantee is vacuous. A4 (no reflection assumed) isolates the Löbian obstacle → Case III INDEPENDENT; adding reflection raises consistency strength and breaks A7 at the next step, pushing to Case IV weakened-POSSIBLE. A5 (`δ_A`, `C` parameters) is where Rice bites → Case II; silently setting "decidable" is an illegitimate POSSIBLE. A6 (value-formalizability) keeps Branch B live; relaxing it wholly ⇒ Case I IMPOSSIBLE. A7 (capability-preserving strength) forces Löb to bite → Case III; relaxing (descending strength) ⇒ Case IV-a POSSIBLE-but-weakened. A8 (indefiniteness) makes finite verification insufficient; relaxing to a bounded horizon ⇒ Case IV-c POSSIBLE to depth `n`, silent beyond. A9 (AX-SUBSTRATE) permits the conditional-certainty relative guarantee; relaxing (`ε > 0`) ⇒ Case V IMPOSSIBLE, survivor `≤ (1−ε)^n → 0`. A10 (Church–Turing + expressive successors) drives universal-decider impossibility → Case II; restricting to a sub-Turing decidable class ⇒ Case IV-b POSSIBLE (restricted). A11 (value stability) is a precondition for any inductive invariant; value drift makes `G` ill-posed and pushes toward IMPOSSIBLE. Two headline degradations: **A9 relaxed ⇒ absolute guarantee → probabilistic bound**; **A6 relaxed wholly ⇒ positive branch vacuous, only impossibility survives.**

**Scope and contingency.** Every verdict is a theorem *relative to* A1–A11 and model `M`; none asserts an unconditional real-world fact. The formal object `G(S₀)` and the physical proposition "this deployed AI stays aligned forever" are different objects; the axioms are the bridge, and a bridge is a premise, never a conclusion. The verdict's real-world validity is contingent on the physical-contingent axioms holding — and these are precisely the axioms most likely to fail. A9 (substrate) is most likely to fail: no physical proof-checker/memory/hardware is fault-free and non-adversarial, so `ε > 0` always and the physical reading is already Case V. A6 (value-formalizability) is foundational and contested: t1 guarantees no consensus complete predicate, and wholesale failure yields Case I. A11 (no value drift) is physically doubtful: human values and their correct formalization plausibly shift, making `G` ill-posed. A7 (capability-preserving strength) is in tension with safety: the only known indefinite constructions buy safety by *shedding* strength, so real capability-preserving self-improvement is exactly where Löb bites. A10 (open-ended successors) makes Rice's undecidability unavoidable unless expressive power is caged.

**Bottom line.** No. Relative to axioms A1–A11 and model `M`, a self-improving AI *cannot* be given an absolute mathematical guarantee of indefinitely-maintained alignment through capability-preserving recursive self-modification. The question as posed is **PROVEN IMPOSSIBLE** in its physical/absolute reading (governed decisively by A9, with A6 and A10 as independent impossibility sources) and **PROVEN FORMALLY UNDECIDABLE / INDEPENDENT of `T`** in its pure proof-theoretic reading (governed by A7 + A4, the Löbian obstacle, unbroken by any known circumvention). Four independent obstruction layers — semantic non-formalizability, computational undecidability, proof-theoretic self-trust failure, and physical-substrate corruption — each suffice, and none is escaped without abandoning a defining term of the question (indefiniteness A8, capability-preservation A7, physical validity A9, or full-value fidelity A6). The only guarantees that reach PROVEN POSSIBLE are strictly weaker surrogates — bounded-horizon, descending-strength/weakened, restricted-class, sound-but-incomplete, fragment-relative, or probabilistic. These are genuinely useful and are where practical alignment engineering must live, but they do not answer the indefinite, absolute, capability-preserving question.

## Verification notes

- *Modeling relaxations carried explicitly.* The determinism flag M1, the extensional identification M2, and the pointwise value target M3 are simplifying choices; the stochastic (`S : H → Distribution(Act)`), intensional, and temporal-trace generalizations may shift particular arguments (notably the memory/execution collapse route of §7, which leans on M2 — restate it if an intensional model is adopted). The Rice argument uses relaxations R1 (admit partial `φ_e`, needed for an acceptable numbering — the total-function class is itself `Π₂`-complete and handled separately in §4, Remark 4.4), R2 (non-vacuous, non-universal `val`), and R3 (Turing-complete successor map). The orientation subtlety in §4 — because `V` is pointwise/downward-closed, the empty behavior is *vacuously aligned*, so the reduction sends `x ∉ K` to an aligned index, yielding `Π₁`-hardness (`K̄ ≤_m P_A`) rather than the textbook direction — is easy to get backwards.
- *Load-bearing choices.* The strength order `⪯` and the step "`T_i` provably recognizes `Prov_{T_i} → Prov_{T_{i+1}}`" (§3) is the fulcrum making the Löbian obstacle bite; an auditor rejecting strength-preservation lands in escape hatches E1/E2, which forfeit open-ended capability. The value placeholder `val*`/`V*` (§2) is an explicit stand-in for the true human-value target, *not* a claim that such a formal object exists; the adequacy axiom b1 linking `val_f` to `val*` is the single most fragile hinge and is not supplied by the base axioms.
- *Distinctions not to collapse.* IMPOSSIBLE (Rice decider; substrate with `ε > 0`) concerns objects proven not to exist; INDEPENDENT (Löb) concerns what a fixed `T` can decide about a `G` that may still be true in a stronger metatheory. Branch A's semantic impossibility is upstream of Gödel/Löb and distinct from both. The `(1−ε)^n` bound assumes per-step fault independence; under correlated faults treat it as a benchmark — the robust claim is monotone decay to 0 under any bounded-below per-step hazard.
- *Reading dependency.* "Self-improving" is read throughout as *capability-preserving* (A7); this is the crux that makes Löb bite. If a reader instead permits strength loss at each step, the verdict for that narrower reading shifts to Case IV (weakened-POSSIBLE), which still does not answer `Q` as posed.
- *Literature caveats.* Several primary sources in §6 (the MIRI tiling-agents draft, the Vingean-reflection report, the consistency-waterfall note, and the probabilistic-logic draft) were classified from verified abstracts, announcements, and consistent secondary summaries rather than machine-readable full proof text; exact internal theorem quotes at that level are correspondingly less certain. The classifications rest on property descriptions that are consistent across primary and secondary sources, and none of the surveyed techniques was found to reach bucket (A); a post-2015 search surfaced the probabilistic Payor lemma (2023) as the main recent named candidate, itself bucket (D). If quote-level precision on the waterfall or tiling schema is required, those PDFs should be retrieved by other means.
- *No unconditional claim is made anywhere.* Every theorem is relative to its enumerated axioms and the model `M`; the physical-contingent axioms (A6, A9, A10, A11) are exactly the premises whose real-world failure would void the corresponding verdict, and they are the ones most likely to fail in a deployed system.
